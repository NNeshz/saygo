### Módulo 1 — Puentes y Repaso Estratégico

*Cimentando el A1 · 6 horas*

**Diagnóstico activo de A1**

- Presente Simple vs. Continuo: ¿hábito o acción de ahora?
- Pasado Simple: narrar el día de ayer en 5 oraciones sin errores de DID
- Futuros: diferenciar plan (going to) de decisión/promesa espontánea (will)

**Vocabulario de identidad mexicana**

- Nacionalidad y origen: I am Mexican / I am from Mexico City / I come from Guadalajara
- Lugares: pyramid, colonial city, downtown/zócalo, plaza, cathedral, museum
- Comida: taco, tortilla, tamale, enchilada, mole, guacamole, salsa, spicy/hot, corn, avocado
- Celebraciones: Day of the Dead, posada, quinceañera (sweet fifteen), piñata, mariachi

**Errores comunes español → inglés**

- "I have years" → I am 15 years old
- "I am agree" → I agree / I don't agree
- "People is" → People are
- "Explain me" → Explain to me / Tell me
- "I am interesting" → I am interested
- "I have hunger/cold/heat" → I am hungry / I am cold / I am hot
- "Make me a favor" → Do me a favor
- "I go to the school every day" → I go to school every day (sin artículo)
- "I am having 15 years" → I am 15 years old (no continuo con estados)

### Módulo 2 — Comparativos y Superlativos

*Descripción y comparación avanzada · 8 horas*

**Comparativos de superioridad e inferioridad**

- Adjetivos cortos: tall → taller than / short → shorter than / big → bigger than
- Adjetivos largos: more beautiful than / more expensive than / more interesting than
- Inferioridad: less boring than / less difficult than / less expensive than
- Igualdad: as tall as / as good as / not as modern as

**Superlativos**

- Adjetivos cortos: the tallest, the biggest, the hottest
- Adjetivos largos: the most beautiful, the most important
- Irregulares: the best, the worst, the farthest/furthest
- Con preposición "in": the best student in the class / the biggest city in Mexico

**Comparativos y superlativos con sustantivos**

- more money than / fewer people than / less water than
- the most people / the least time

**Aplicación al contexto escolar mexicano**

- Public school is less expensive than private school
- Math is more difficult than Art / History is the most interesting subject
- Mexico City is bigger than Mérida / Guadalajara has the best weather in Mexico
- In Mexico, high school lasts 3 years. In the US, it lasts 4 years.

**Estructuras de opinión comparativa**

- I think (that)... is better than...
- In my opinion, ... is the most...
- What do you think about...?

### Módulo 3 — Narrativa en el Pasado

*Pasado Continuo: was/were + -ing · 10 horas*

**Formación del Pasado Continuo**

- Afirmativo: I was studying / They were playing / She was working
- Negativo: I wasn't sleeping / We weren't watching TV
- Interrogativo: Were you listening? / Was he driving?
- Respuestas cortas: Yes, I was / No, they weren't

**Dos usos principales**

- Acción interrumpida: I was doing my homework when the lights went out
- Dos acciones simultáneas: While I was studying, my brother was playing video games
- Contexto de historia: It was raining. The wind was blowing. Suddenly...

**While vs. When**

- While + Pasado Continuo (acción en desarrollo): While I was walking...
- When + Pasado Simple (interrupción): ...when I saw my teacher

**Narrativa personal y académica**

- I was running in the hallway when I fell
- Everyone was silent. The teacher was watching us.
- During the posada, everyone was singing. Suddenly, the piñata broke.

**Vocabulario de narración**

- suddenly, immediately, at that moment, in the end, finally, luckily, unfortunately, while, during

### Módulo 4 — Presente Perfecto

*Experiencias y cambios · have/has + participio · 12 horas*

**Formación**

- Afirmativo: I have visited / She has eaten / We have studied
- Negativo: I haven't been / He hasn't finished
- Interrogativo: Have you seen...? / Has she called...?
- Respuestas cortas: Yes, I have / No, he hasn't

**Participios irregulares esenciales**

- be → been / go → gone/been / see → seen / do → done / eat → eaten
- write → written / read → read / take → taken / give → given / find → found
- break → broken / choose → chosen / forget → forgotten / lose → lost

**Usos principales en A2**

- Experiencias de vida (sin tiempo específico): I have been to Cancún / Have you ever eaten tamales?
- Cambios recientes: She has changed a lot / My school has improved
- Acciones que empezaron y continúan: I have lived here for 5 years / She has studied English since 2020
- Acciones recientes con relevancia ahora: I have lost my ID card (and I still don't have it)

**For vs. Since vs. Ago — contraste sistemático**

- for + período de tiempo (duración) → Presente Perfecto: for two years, for six months
- since + punto de inicio → Presente Perfecto: since 2020, since Monday, since I was a child
- ago + Pasado Simple (distancia desde hoy): two years ago, a week ago
- "I have arrived two years ago" ❌ → "I arrived two years ago" ✓
- "I live here since 5 years" ❌ → "I have lived here for 5 years / since 2019" ✓

**Marcadores temporales clave**

- Ever / Never: Have you ever...? / I have never...
- Already / Yet: I have already finished / Have you finished yet?
- Just: I have just arrived
- Recently / Lately: I haven't seen her recently

**Contraste Pasado Simple vs. Presente Perfecto**

- I went to Cancún last year (tiempo terminado) vs. I have been to Cancún (experiencia)
- Did you eat? (tiempo específico) vs. Have you eaten? (sin especificar cuándo)

**Aplicación práctica**

- CV básico: I have worked at... / I have studied... for... years
- Trámites: I have lost my passport / Have you received my documents?
- Escuela: I haven't done my homework yet / Have you already registered for the exam?

### Módulo 5 — Modales Avanzados

*Obligación, posibilidad y consejo · 10 horas*

**MUST — obligación fuerte y deducción**

- Obligación interna: I must study for the exam
- Deducción lógica: He must be tired (seguramente está cansado)
- Prohibición: You mustn't use your phone in class

**HAVE TO — obligación externa**

- Normas y reglas: I have to wear a uniform / Students have to arrive on time
- Trámites: I have to renew my passport / You have to fill out this form
- Diferencia: must = sentida internamente / have to = norma externa

**NEED TO — necesidad cotidiana**

- Necesidad neutra y frecuente en conversación: I need to call my mom / You need to bring your ID
- Negativo: You don't need to bring cash / I don't need to wear a tie

**SHOULD — consejo y recomendación**

- Consejo: You should study more / She should see a doctor
- Reproche suave: You shouldn't eat so much spicy food
- Expectativa: The bus should arrive at 5:00

**MIGHT — posibilidad débil**

- Posibilidad incierta: It might rain tomorrow / I might go to the party
- Diferencia con may: sinónimos, pero might es más incierto

**COULD — habilidad pasada, petición, sugerencia**

- Habilidad pasada: I could swim when I was five
- Petición educada: Could you help me? / Could you repeat that?
- Sugerencia: We could study at the library

**BE ABLE TO — alternativa a CAN**

- Futuro: I will be able to help you tomorrow — "I will can" ❌
- Pasado con logro específico: I was able to finish the exam on time
- could (habilidad general pasada) vs was/were able to (logro específico puntual)

**Aplicación al contexto mexicano**

- Reglamentos: You mustn't cheat / You have to submit assignments on Fridays
- Trámites: You have to bring your CURP and birth certificate
- Salud: You should drink more water / It might be the altitude
- Transporte: We could take the metro / The bus might be late because of traffic

### Módulo 6 — El Condicional Básico

*Zero Conditional + First Conditional · 8 horas*

**Zero Conditional — verdades generales**

- Estructura: If + Presente Simple, Presente Simple
- If you heat water to 100°C, it boils / If you don't study, you fail
- Uso: hechos científicos, reglas generales, consejos universales

**First Conditional — futuro real y probable**

- Estructura: If + Presente Simple, will + infinitivo
- If it rains tomorrow, I will stay home / If I pass the exam, I will celebrate
- Variaciones con modales: If you finish early, you can leave / If you are sick, you should rest
- Variaciones con imperativos: If you see the teacher, tell him I am sick

**When vs. If en futuros**

- if = condición incierta (puede o no pasar): If it rains, I will stay home
- when = evento seguro, solo incierto el momento: When I graduate, I will look for a job
- "When you will arrive, call me" ❌ → "When you arrive, call me" ✓
- Regla: después de when/if de futuro → Presente Simple (nunca will)

**Unless — a menos que**

- Unless you study, you won't pass = If you don't study, you won't pass

**As soon as — en cuanto**

- I will call you as soon as I arrive / As soon as the class finishes, we will go to the cafeteria

**"In case" — por si acaso**

- Estructura: in case + Presente Simple (precaución previa)
- Take an umbrella in case it rains / Bring your CURP in case they ask for it
- Diferencia: If it rains, take an umbrella (instrucción) vs Take an umbrella in case it rains (precaución)

**Aplicaciones prácticas**

- If I get a scholarship, I will study medicine
- If the metro is full, we will take the bus
- If you don't bring the receipt, they won't give you the document

### Módulo 7 — Voz Pasiva Básica

*Presente y Pasado Simple · 8 horas*

**Estructura básica**

- Presente Simple: The homework is checked by the teacher / English is spoken here
- Pasado Simple: The window was broken by a student / The exam was cancelled
- Negativo: The assignment isn't finished / The meeting wasn't recorded
- Interrogativo: Was the homework collected? / Is Spanish taught in this school?

**"By + agente" (opcional en A2)**

- The cake was made by my mother
- Mexico was conquered by Spain (contexto histórico básico)

**Verbos comunes en voz pasiva**

- make → made / build → built / write → written / invent → invented / discover → discovered
- cancel → cancelled / postpone → postponed / approve → approved / reject → rejected

**Diferencia activa vs. pasiva**

- The teacher graded the exam (activa) vs. The exam was graded by the teacher (pasiva)

**Aplicación práctica**

- Escuela: The exam was postponed / My essay was graded / The students were selected
- Noticias: The road was closed / A new school was built in my neighborhood
- Trámites: The application was approved / My ID card was issued last week
- Tecnología: The message was sent / The file was downloaded

### Módulo 8 — Pronombres Reflexivos y Recíprocos

*Reflexión y reciprocidad · 6 horas*

**Pronombres reflexivos**

- I → myself / You → yourself / He → himself / She → herself / It → itself
- We → ourselves / They → themselves

**Usos en A2**

- Acción reflexiva: I wash myself / She looks at herself in the mirror
- Énfasis (solito/a): I did it myself / The homework was done by the students themselves
- Verbos comunes: enjoy yourself, help yourself, behave yourself, hurt yourself, teach yourself

**"On one's own" — variante cotidiana**

- I did it on my own / She lives on her own (sinónimo frecuente de by myself/herself)

**Pronombres recíprocos**

- each other: My classmates and I help each other / They love each other
- one another: The teachers respect one another (sinónimo formal)

**Aplicación cultural**

- Familia mexicana: We help each other / We take care of each other
- Trabajo en equipo: We taught each other / The group organized itself

### Módulo 9 — Reported Speech Básico

*Say, Tell, Ask · 8 horas*

**Say vs. Tell vs. Ask**

- Say (sin objeto indirecto obligatorio): He said (that) he was tired
- Tell + objeto indirecto: He told me (that) he was tired / She told us a story
- Ask: He asked me if I was ready / She asked where the bathroom was

**Reported Speech con THAT (opcional)**

- "I am sick" → He said (that) he was sick
- "I will call you" → She said (that) she would call me
- "I have finished" → He said (that) he had finished

**Cambios básicos (backshift)**

- Presente Simple → Pasado Simple: "I live here" → He said he lived there
- Will → Would: "I will go" → She said she would go
- Can → Could: "I can swim" → He said he could swim
- Cambios de pronombres y adverbios: now → then, today → that day, here → there, this → that

**Preguntas reportadas**

- Yes/No: "Are you Mexican?" → He asked if I was Mexican
- WH-: "Where do you live?" → She asked where I lived
- "He asked where did I live" ❌ → "He asked where I lived" ✓ (sin inversión)

**Órdenes y peticiones reportadas**

- "Sit down" → The teacher told us to sit down
- "Don't be late" → He told me not to be late

**Verbos de reporte adicionales**

- advise + to: The doctor advised me to rest / She advised us to arrive early
- warn + not to: He warned me not to forget my ID / She warned us not to be late
- suggest + -ing: She suggested taking the metro / He suggested arriving early
- remind + to: She reminded me to bring the receipt

### Módulo 10 — Phrasal Verbs Esenciales

*El inglés cotidiano real · 10 horas*

**Lógica de las partículas — no memorizar de cero**

- up = completar/terminar/aumentar: finish up, use up, give up, grow up
- out = hacia afuera/completamente: find out, run out, go out, drop out
- back = retorno/repetición: call back, come back, pay back, go back
- down = reducir/registrar: write down, calm down, cut down
- away = alejamiento/desaparición: go away, throw away, put away
- Nota: la lógica no aplica al 100%, pero reduce la memorización a la mitad

**Separables vs. inseparables (concepto)**

- Inseparables: look for, look after, get on, get off, run out of
- Separables: turn on/off, put on, take off, fill out, pick up, drop off, call back, hang up

**Phrasal verbs por categoría**

- Tecnología: turn on/off, log in, log out, set up, back up, charge up, run out of battery
- Ropa: put on, take off, try on, dress up
- Búsqueda: look for, find out, figure out
- Social: hang out with, catch up, call back, pick up (the phone), hang up
- Escuela/trabajo: fill out (a form), hand in, drop out, keep up with, give up, catch up on
- Transporte: get on, get off, pick up, drop off
- Relaciones: get along with, break up with, make up

**Sustantivos derivados**

- a breakup, a makeup, a try-on, a dropout, a backup

**Aplicación al contexto mexicano**

- "I have to fill out the form for my scholarship"
- "The metro was full, so I got off at the next station"
- "We hung out at the plaza downtown"
- "I ran out of money / data / gas / time"

### Módulo 11 — Conectores del Discurso

*Oraciones aisladas → párrafos coherentes · 8 horas*

**Adición y énfasis**

- In addition, furthermore, moreover, besides, also, as well as, too
- The school has a new library. In addition, the laboratories were renovated.

**Contraste y concesión**

- However, although, though, even though, but, yet, on the other hand, in contrast, while, whereas
- Although the exam was difficult, I passed / The private school is expensive. However, it offers scholarships.

**Causa y resultado**

- Because, since, as, because of, due to, therefore, as a result, consequently, so
- I didn't study. As a result, I failed the exam.

**Ejemplificación**

- For example, for instance, such as, like, especially, particularly
- I love Mexican food, such as tacos, tamales, and pozole.

**Secuencia y tiempo**

- First(ly), second(ly), then, next, after that, finally, eventually, in the end, meanwhile
- First, you register online. Then, you pay the tuition. Finally, you get your schedule.

**Condición**

- Otherwise, if not, unless, as long as, provided that
- You must bring your ID. Otherwise, you won't be allowed to enter.

**Propósito y finalidad — To / For / In order to / So that**

- to + infinitivo (el más frecuente): I study English to get a better job / I went to the store to buy milk
- in order to (más formal): I wake up early in order to avoid traffic
- for + sustantivo: This form is for registration / I came for help
- so that + cláusula: I study so that I can pass the exam
- "I study English for improve my skills" ❌ → "I study English to improve my skills" ✓
- "I went there for to buy food" ❌ → "I went there to buy food" ✓

**Práctica de escritura**

- "My ideal school" — párrafo 80–100 palabras usando mínimo 5 conectores diferentes
- Comparar dos universidades mexicanas usando conectores de contraste

### Módulo 12 — Inglés para la Vida Real en México

*Funciones comunicativas avanzadas A2 · 14 horas*

**Educación y escuela**

- Sistema educativo: elementary school, middle school, high school, college/university, major, degree
- Trámites: semester, tuition, scholarship, enrollment, registration, transcript, attendance, GPA
- Aula: assignment, project, essay, presentation, thesis, internship, extracurricular activities, graduation, diploma, valedictorian
- "I didn't catch that. Could you repeat?" / "I don't follow. Can you explain?" / "What does ... mean?"
- "I am struggling with..." / "Could you give me an extension?" / "Is there extra credit?"

**Trámites y documentos oficiales**

- Identidad: birth certificate, CURP, INE/IFE (voter ID), passport, visa, proof of address
- Banca: savings account, debit/credit card, balance, deposit, withdrawal, transfer, ATM, PIN, statement, loan
- Procesos: fill out a form, make an appointment, stand in line/queue, submit documents, official stamp, signature
- "I would like to apply for..." / "What documents do I need?" / "How long does it take?"

**Salud y emergencias**

- Síntomas: fever, cough, sore throat, runny nose, headache, stomachache, back pain, allergy, rash, sprain, fracture, dizziness, nausea
- Partes avanzadas: chest, back, knee, ankle, wrist, elbow, shoulder, hip, throat, lung, heart, bone, muscle, skin
- Farmacia: prescription, over-the-counter, drops, pills, syrup, cream, bandage, antiseptic, vaccine, antibiotic, painkiller
- "It hurts when I..." / "I have been feeling sick for..." / "I am allergic to..."

**Tecnología y medios digitales**

- Hardware: laptop, desktop, tablet, smartphone, charger, headphones, speaker, keyboard, mouse, printer, USB
- Acciones: install, update, download, upload, delete, save, share, search, stream, back up, crash, restart
- Redes: WiFi, password, app, social media, post, comment, like, follow, block, report, subscribe, spam, virus, hack
- "My phone died" / "I have no signal" / "The WiFi is down" / "My account was hacked"

**Transporte y movilidad urbana**

- Público: metro, subway station, platform, turnstile, bus stop, metrobus, colectivo, shared taxi, RTP
- Privado: motorcycle, scooter, bicycle, Uber/Didi, taxi
- Infraestructura: highway, toll booth, speed bump (topes), intersection, traffic light, roundabout, bridge, parking lot
- "How much is the fare?" / "Does this bus go to...?" / "I need to get off at the next stop"

**Vivienda y servicios**

- Renta: landlord/landlady, tenant, lease, contract, deposit, monthly rent, utilities, maintenance
- Servicios: water, electricity, gas, internet, cable TV, garbage collection, property tax
- "The sink is clogged" / "We had a power outage" / "The rent is due on the 1st"

### Módulo 13 — Consolidación A2 y Proyectos Finales

*De la teoría a la acción · 10 horas*

**Proyecto 1 — Perfil académico y profesional**

- Redactar un CV básico en inglés usando Presente Perfecto y Pasado Simple
- Simular entrevista para beca o trabajo: "Tell me about yourself" / "What are your strengths?" / "Why should we choose you?"

**Proyecto 2 — Presentación oral (3–5 min)**

- "A Mexican tradition that the world should know" (Día de Muertos, una receta, una celebración local)
- Uso obligatorio de conectores, comparativos, superlativos y modales

**Proyecto 3 — Simulacro de trámite completo**

- Escenario: abrir una cuenta bancaria, inscribirse a una universidad, o ir al doctor
- 10 frases esenciales, 5 preguntas clave y las respuestas esperadas

**Proyecto 4 — Narrativa creativa**

- Cuento corto 150–200 palabras: Pasado Simple, Pasado Continuo, dos modales y tres conectores
- Contextos: anécdota escolar, viaje en transporte público, fiesta familiar

**Evaluación de competencias A2**

- Comprensión lectora: artículo sobre educación o tecnología + preguntas
- Comprensión auditiva: instrucciones básicas, anuncios de aeropuerto/estación, diálogos de trámites
- Expresión escrita: email formal (a profesor o institución) y uno informal (a un amigo)
- Expresión oral: describir una foto, comparar dos opciones, narrar una experiencia personal

**Repertorio cultural — cómo explicar México al mundo**

- "How to explain Mexican..." food, family, holidays, school system, geography en 3 minutos
- "It's similar to... but different because..." / "The best way to describe it is..." / "You could think of it as..."
