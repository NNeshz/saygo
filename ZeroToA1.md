### Módulo 1 — Fonética Fundamental

*El alfabeto sonoro · 6–8 horas*

**Sonidos vocálicos**

- /æ/ vs /ʌ/ — cat vs cup
- /ɪ/ vs /iː/ — sit vs sheep
- /ʊ/ vs /uː/ — book vs food
- /ɑː/ /ɔː/ /ɜː/ — car, door, bird
- /ə/ la schwa — sobre, banana, teacher

**Consonantes problemáticas**

- /θ/ unvoiced (think) vs /ð/ voiced (this)
- /ʃ/ (shop), /ʒ/ (television), /ŋ/ (sing)
- /w/ vs /v/ — west vs vest

**Reglas básicas**

- Diferencia sonidos cortos y largos
- Acentuación en palabras de 2 sílabas: TA-ble vs be-GIN
- Pronunciación de -s final: /s/, /z/, /ɪz/

**Alfabeto y deletreo**

- Alfabeto completo A–Z
- Uso práctico: "How do you spell...?"

### Módulo 2 — TO BE Afirmativo y Presentaciones

*Primer contacto · 8 horas*

**Pronombres personales sujeto**

- I, you, he, she, it, we, they — memorización absoluta

**TO BE presente afirmativo**

- I am (I'm) / You are (You're) / He is (He's) / She is (She's)
- It is (It's) / We are (We're) / They are (They're)
- Contracciones: uso en inglés oral vs. escrito formal

**Saludos y despedidas**

- Hello, Hi, Good morning / afternoon / evening / night
- Bye, See you later, Nice to meet you

**Presentaciones básicas**

- My name is... / I'm...
- I am from... (país) / I am [nacionalidad]

**Números 0–100**

- Para edades, cantidades básicas y deletreo numérico

### Módulo 3 — TO BE Completo + Posesivos + WH-

*Información personal y preguntas esenciales · 10–12 horas*

**TO BE negativo e interrogativo**

- Negativo: I'm not / You aren't / He isn't / She isn't / We aren't / They aren't
- Interrogativo: Am I...? / Are you...? / Is he/she/it...? / Are we/they...?
- Respuestas cortas: Yes, I am / No, I'm not / Yes, he is / No, he isn't

**Adjetivos posesivos**

- My, Your, His, Her, Its, Our, Their
- Aplicación: My name, Your book, His car, Her mother, Our house

**WH- con TO BE (primer grupo)**

- What is your name? / What is this/that?
- Where are you from? / Where is he/she?
- Who is he/she? / Who are they?
- How are you? (Fine, thanks)
- How old are you? / How old is he/she?
- Which color do you prefer, red or blue?

### Módulo 4 — Descripción Física y Espacial

*HOW + adjetivo · demostrativos · have/has · 10 horas*

**Have / Has (posesión básica)**

- I have / You have / We have / They have
- He has / She has / It has
- I have a car / She has two brothers

**Adjetivos de apariencia y descripción**

- Altura: tall, short — Peso: fat, thin, slim, heavy — Edad: young, old
- Partes del cuerpo: head, hair, eyes, nose, mouth, ears, arms, hands, legs, feet
- Colores: red, blue, green, yellow, black, white, brown, grey, pink, orange, purple

**Comparativos básicos**

- tall → taller than / short → shorter than / big → bigger than
- He is taller than me / My house is bigger than your house

**Demostrativos**

- This (singular cercano) / That (singular lejano)
- These (plural cercano) / Those (plural lejano)

**Preposiciones de lugar**

- in, on, under, next to, behind, in front of, between

**HOW + adjetivo (segundo grupo WH-)**

- How tall is he/she? / How big is your house? / How old is your brother?

**Adverbios de intensidad**

- Very (intensificador neutro): very tall, very good, very happy
- Too (exceso negativo): too expensive (I can't buy it), too hot (uncomfortable)
- Distinción clave: "very big" (aceptable) vs "too big" (problemático)

### Módulo 5 — Plurales, Artículos, There is/are y Quantifiers

*El entorno y la existencia · 10 horas*

**Pluralización**

- Regla general +s: book → books
- +es (s, sh, ch, x, o): box → boxes, potato → potatoes
- Consonante + y → -ies: baby → babies
- Irregulares: man/men, woman/women, child/children, person/people, foot/feet, tooth/teeth

**Artículos**

- Indefinidos: a / an (uso fonético: a university, an hour)
- Definido: the — específico/conocido

**There is / There are**

- Afirmativo: There is a book / There are two books
- Negativo: There isn't a pen / There aren't any books
- Interrogativo: Is there...? / Are there...?
- Respuestas cortas: Yes, there is / No, there aren't

**Some y Any**

- Some en afirmativas: There are some chairs / I have some water
- Any en negativas e interrogativas: There aren't any books / Are there any students?

**Quantifiers básicos**

- a lot of + contables e incontables: a lot of people, a lot of time
- a few + contables: a few books, a few friends (algo, positivo)
- a little + incontables: a little water, a little time (algo, positivo)
- enough: There is enough water / There aren't enough chairs
- too many + contables: too many students (exceso negativo)
- too few + contables: too few chairs (insuficiencia negativa)

**Possessive 's (genitivo sajón)**

- John's car / My mother's sister / The teacher's book
- Singular + 's + objeto poseído — contraste con pronombres posesivos

**Vocabulario del aula/casa**

- book, pen, table, chair, door, window, bag, phone, computer, desk, room

### Módulo 6 — Posesión y Familia

*Have/has · have got · vocabulario familiar · 8–10 horas*

**Vocabulario familiar**

- mother, father, parents, sister, brother, siblings, grandmother, grandfather, grandparents
- daughter, son, children, wife, husband, baby

**Have / Has (repaso y ampliación)**

- My mother has two sisters. I don't have any brothers.

**Have Got (variante británica)**

- I've got / You haven't got / Has she got...?
- Equivalente a have/has — reconocimiento auditivo

**Much / Many / A lot of**

- many + contables: many books, many friends
- much + incontables: much water, much money (principalmente en negativas e interrogativas)
- a lot of + ambos: a lot of people, a lot of time

**"For" de duración — primer contacto**

- I studied for two hours / She waited for a long time
- I have lived here for five years (solo reconocimiento; producción plena en A2)
- Nota: "for" + período de tiempo; no confundir con "since" + punto de inicio

### Módulo 7 — Rutinas Diarias

*Presente Simple afirmativo · 10–12 horas*

**Verbos de acción cotidiana**

- get up, wake up, have breakfast/lunch/dinner, go to work/school
- start, finish, work, study, eat, drink, watch TV, read, write, sleep, go to bed, walk, run

**Marcadores de tiempo**

- in the morning, in the afternoon, in the evening, at night
- every day, on Monday/Tuesday... (días completos)
- Días: Monday–Sunday / Meses: January–December / Estaciones: spring, summer, autumn/fall, winter

**Preposiciones de tiempo**

- at + hora: at 3 o'clock, at noon, at night
- on + día/fecha: on Monday, on Christmas Day
- in + período: in the morning, in July, in summer, in 2026

**Presente Simple afirmativo**

- I/You/We/They + verbo base: I work, They eat
- He/She/It + verbo + s/es: He works, She watches, It goes
- Reglas de -s/-es: +s general; +es (s, sh, ch, x, o, z); consonante+y → -ies

**Adverbios de frecuencia**

- always, usually, often, sometimes, never
- Posición: antes del verbo principal (I always eat) / después de TO BE (I am always tired)

**Horas básicas**

- o'clock, half past, quarter past/to

**"To" de dirección y "For" de propósito — primer contacto**

- TO + lugar (movimiento): I go to school / I walk to the store
- Excepciones sin "to": I go home ✓ / I go there ✓ — nunca "go to home / go to there"
- FOR + sustantivo (propósito): I study for the exam / I wait for the bus
- FOR + beneficiario: I made this for you / a gift for my mother
- TO + destinatario: Give it to me / Talk to her / Listen to the teacher

### Módulo 8 — Hábitos, Negaciones y Preguntas

*Presente Simple completo · WH- con DO/DOES · 12–14 horas*

**Presente Simple negativo e interrogativo**

- Negativo: I/You/We/They + don't + infinitivo / He/She/It + doesn't + infinitivo
- Interrogativo: Do + I/you/we/they + infinitivo? / Does + he/she/it + infinitivo?
- Respuestas cortas: Yes, I do / No, he doesn't

**WH- con DO/DOES (tercer grupo)**

- What do you do? (profesión)
- Where does he work? / When do you get up? / Why do you study English? + Because...
- How do you go to school? (by bus, on foot, by car)
- How many + sustantivo plural: How many brothers do you have?

**Preguntas de sujeto vs. preguntas de objeto**

- Pregunta de objeto (con DO/DOES): Who do you like? → I like María
- Pregunta de sujeto (sin DO/DOES): Who likes pizza? → John likes pizza
- Regla: si el WH- reemplaza al sujeto, NO se usa DO/DOES ni inversión
- Ejemplos: Who works here? / What happens next? / Who called you?
- "Who does go to school?" ❌ → "Who goes to school?" ✓

**How often — frecuencia**

- How often do you...?
- Once a week / Twice a month / Every day / Never / Always

**Conectores básicos**

- and (adición): I like pizza and pasta
- but (contraste): I like pizza but I don't like cheese
- because (causa): I study English because I want to travel
- so (resultado): I was tired so I went to bed early

**Actividades de tiempo libre**

- play football, listen to music, play video games, swim, dance, sing

---

### Módulo 9 — Capacidades, Preferencias y Verbos + Infinitivo

*CAN · LIKE · Want/Need/Plan + to · 8–10 horas*

**Modal CAN**

- Afirmativo: I can swim / He can play the guitar
- Negativo: I can't drive / She can't cook
- Interrogativo: Can you...? / Can he...? — Respuestas: Yes, I can / No, I can't

**LIKE + gustos**

- like + sustantivo: I like pizza / I don't like spiders
- like + verbo -ing: I like swimming / She likes reading
- Escala: love → like → don't like → hate

**WOULD LIKE — cortesía**

- I'd like a coffee (quiero ahora) vs. I like coffee (me gusta en general)

**Verbos seguidos de infinitivo (to + verbo)**

- want to, need to, decide to, plan to, try to, forget to, remember to
- I want to travel / She needs to study / We decided to stay
- Contraste: like + -ing (gusto general) vs want + to (deseo específico)
- I like swimming (me gusta nadar) vs I want to swim today (quiero nadar hoy)
- "I want swimming" ❌ → "I want to swim" ✓

**Vocabulario de capacidades**

- Deportes, instrumentos musicales (play the guitar/piano — artículo "the"), idiomas (speak English)

### Módulo 10 — Presente Continuo

*Acciones en el momento · 8–10 horas*

**Formación**

- am / is / are + verbo + -ing
- I am eating / He is reading / They are playing

**Reglas ortográficas (-ing)**

- General: add -ing (play → playing)
- Sílaba tónica CVC: run → running, swim → swimming
- e final: make → making, write → writing
- ie final: lie → lying

**Uso principal y preguntas**

- Acciones que ocurren ahora mismo
- What are you doing? — I'm watching TV.
- Is he sleeping? — Yes, he is / No, he isn't

**Contraste Presente Simple vs. Continuo**

- I eat pizza (hábito general) vs. I'm eating pizza (ahora mismo)
- He works here (empleo) vs. He is working now (acción actual)

**Verbos de acción inmediata**

- eat, drink, read, write, watch, play, talk, listen, run, sleep, cook, clean, wait

---

### Módulo 11 — Pronombres Objeto

*Object pronouns · acuerdo y afiliación · 6–8 horas*

**Pronombres objeto**

- I → me / You → you / He → him / She → her / It → it / We → us / They → them
- Uso después de verbos: I like him / She can see me / We have them
- Uso después de preposiciones: with me, to you, for her, about us, next to them
- Distinción sujeto vs. objeto: He loves her / She loves him

**Acuerdo y afiliación**

- "Me too" (acuerdo positivo): "I like pizza" → "Me too"
- "Me neither" (acuerdo negativo): "I don't like spiders" → "Me neither"
- "I am too" vs "Me too" — distinción sujeto/objeto en respuestas cortas

---

### Módulo 12 — Adverbios de Manera ✦ nuevo

*Describir cómo se hace algo · 6–8 horas*

**Formación**

- adjetivo + -ly: slow → slowly, careful → carefully, loud → loudly, quick → quickly
- Irregulares esenciales: good → well / hard → hard / fast → fast / early → early (sin cambio)

**Posición en la oración**

- Después del verbo o del objeto directo
- She speaks English well / He drives carefully

**Contraste adjetivo vs. adverbio**

- She is a slow driver (adjetivo — describe al sustantivo)
- She drives slowly (adverbio — describe la acción)
- "She speaks good" ❌ → "She speaks well" ✓

**Aplicación práctica**

- I study hard / He plays football badly / The teacher explains clearly
- She arrived early / They work quickly / He answered correctly

### Módulo 13 — Preguntas WH- Avanzadas + Clima

*Interacción social completa · 10 horas*

**WH- avanzadas**

- How much...? (incontables/precios): How much is it? / How much water do you drink?
- How many...? (contables): How many books do you have?
- Which (elección limitada): Which one do you prefer? / Which car is yours?
- Who (sujeto u objeto): Who can help? / Who did you see?
- What can you do? / Who can play the piano? / How many languages can you speak?

**Let's — sugerencias inmediatas**

- Afirmativo: Let's go! / Let's eat! / Let's study
- Negativo: Let's not fight / Let's not worry

**El clima (Weather)**

- What's the weather like?
- Vocabulario: sunny, rainy, cloudy, windy, hot, cold, warm, cool, snow, snowing
- It's sunny today / It's raining / It's very hot

### Módulo 14 — Pasado Simple

*TO BE · regulares · irregulares · there was/were · 14–16 horas*

**Pasado de TO BE**

- I was / You were / He was / She was / It was / We were / They were
- Negativo: wasn't / weren't — Interrogativo: Was I...? / Were you...?

**There was / There were**

- Afirmativo: There was a problem / There were many people
- Negativo: There wasn't time / There weren't any cars

**Verbos regulares en pasado**

- Formación: infinitivo + -ed (work → worked, play → played)
- Pronunciación: /t/ (worked), /d/ (played), /ɪd/ (wanted, needed)
- Ortografía: study → studied, stop → stopped, live → lived

**Verbos irregulares esenciales**

- go → went / eat → ate / drink → drank / see → saw / buy → bought
- have → had / do → did / get → got / come → came / know → knew
- make → made / read → read / say → said / take → took / give → gave / find → found

**Pasado Simple con DID (negativo e interrogativo)**

- Negativo: I didn't go / She didn't eat / We didn't see
- Interrogativo: Did you go? / Did he eat? / Did they see?
- Regla clave: DID + infinitivo — "Did he went?" ❌ → "Did he go?" ✓

**WH- en pasado**

- Where did you go? / What did she eat? / Who did you see? / How did they travel?

**Marcadores temporales de pasado**

- yesterday, last night/week/month/year, ago (two days ago), when I was young, in 2020

### Módulo 15 — Futuros: Going to y Will

*Tiempo y planificación · 8–10 horas*

**BE GOING TO**

- I'm going to study medicine (plan personal)
- Look! It's going to rain (evidencia visual)
- Negativo: I'm not going to... / He isn't going to...
- Interrogativo: Are you going to...? / Is she going to...?

**WILL**

- I'll get it! (decisión espontánea) / I'll help you (oferta)
- Promesas: I will (I won't) forget
- Negativo: I won't tell anyone — Interrogativo: Will you...? / Will he...?

**Contraste going to vs will**

- Plan decidido antes (going to): I'm going to buy a car
- Decisión en el momento (will): I'll take the blue one

**Expresiones de tiempo futuro**

- tomorrow, tonight, next week/month/year, soon, in two days, the day after tomorrow

**Shall I / Shall we — sugerencias formales**

- Shall I open the window? / Shall we go to the cinema?

### Módulo 16 — Consolidación A1

*Integración práctica y funciones comunicativas · 14–16 horas*

**Direcciones**

- Go straight on, Turn left/right, It's on the left/right
- next to, opposite, between, at the traffic lights

**Compras y ropa**

- Ropa: T-shirt, shoes, jacket, trousers, pants, dress, skirt, coat, socks
- How much is it? / Can I have...? / I'd like... / Can I try it on? / That's expensive/cheap

**Restaurante**

- Comida: rice, chicken, fish, bread, fruit, vegetables, meat, soup, salad, cake, ice cream
- The bill, please / Can I see the menu? / I would like... / Is service included?

**Teléfono básico**

- Hello, this is [nombre] speaking / Can I speak to...? / Speaking / I'll call back later

**Imperativos**

- Afirmativos: Open your book, Listen, Sit down, Stand up, Come in, Wait, Call me
- Negativos: Don't touch, Don't worry, Don't forget
- Con cortesía: Please sit down / Come in, please

**Fechas y tiempo completo**

- Ordinales: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th... 31st
- Today is Monday, April 28th, 2026 / My birthday is on May 5th

**Pronombres posesivos**

- mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs
- This is my book (adjetivo) vs. This book is mine (pronombre)
- Is this yours? / No, it's hers.

**Repaso integrado de preposiciones clave**

- Lugar: in, on, under, next to, behind, in front of, between
- Tiempo: at (hora), on (día/fecha), in (período)
- Dirección: to (go to school) + excepciones: go home, go there — nunca "go to home"
- Propósito: for (study for the exam, wait for the bus)
- Duración: for (for two hours, for a long time)
- Beneficiario: for (I made this for you)
- Destinatario: to (Give it to me / Talk to her / Listen to the teacher)
