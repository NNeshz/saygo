## MÓDULO 1: DESCRIPCIÓN FÍSICA COMPLETA

### 1.1 HAVE / HAS — El Verbo de Posesión

> **Regla de oro:** Usamos Have/Has para hablar de partes del cuerpo, características físicas permanentes y posesión.

#### Estructura

| Sujeto | Verbo | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| I, You, We, They | have | I have blue eyes |
| He, She, It | has | She has long hair |

Con adjetivos: `Sujeto + have/has + adjetivo + parte del cuerpo`

- My brother has curly black hair.
- We have beautiful brown eyes.

### 1.9 VERY vs TOO

- **Very** = intensificador neutro o positivo (muy)
- **Too** = exceso negativo o problemático (demasiado)

| Estructura | Ejemplo | Significado |
|---|---|---|
| Very + adjetivo | The coffee is very hot | El café está muy caliente (se puede beber) |
| Too + adjetivo | The coffee is too hot | El café está demasiado caliente (no se puede beber) |
| Very + adjetivo | She is very tall | Ella es muy alta (solo un dato) |
| Too + adjetivo | The shoes are too big | Los zapatos están demasiado grandes (no sirven) |

#### Más ejemplos

- He is very handsome. (Es muy guapo ✅)
- He is too young to drive. (Es demasiado joven para manejar ❌)
- The bag is very heavy. (La mochila está muy pesada ⚠️)
- The bag is too heavy to carry. (La mochila está demasiado pesada para cargarla ❌)

### 1.11 ESTRUCTURA DE DESCRIPCIÓN COMPLETA (Orden Recomendado)

#### Guion de Ejemplo

| Paso | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Identificar | This is... / These are... | This is my brother |
| 2. Edad y complexión | He/She is... | He is 10 years old and tall |
| 3. Detalles físicos | Have / Has | He has black hair and brown eyes |
| 4. Ubicación / Contexto | Is + preposición | He is in Brazil |

#### Ejemplo Completo

> This is my mother and my father. They are married and they are tall and elderly. My mother has short brown hair, her eyes are blue and she has strong arms. My dad is strong and he has short hair. His hair is grey and he has a muscular build with a big beard. They are in Mexico, living in Tlaxcala City.

### 4.2 ARTÍCULOS (Articles)

#### Artículos indefinidos: A / AN

> Se usa según el sonido inicial, no la letra.

| Artículo | Uso | Ejemplos |
|---|---|---|
| a | Sonido consonántico | a university (iu-ni-ver-si-ti), a house, a book, a car |
| an | Sonido vocálico | an hour (áuer), an apple, an egg, an honest man |

#### Artículo definido: THE

> Se usa para algo específico o ya conocido.

| Uso | Ejemplo |
|---|---|
| Específico | The book on the table is mine |
| Único en el mundo | The sun, the moon |
| Superlativos | The tallest building |
| Ríos, océanos | The Nile, The Pacific |
| Instrumentos musicales | She plays the guitar |

### 4.3 THERE IS / THERE ARE (Existencia)

#### Formas

| Forma | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| Afirmativa | There is a book | There are two books |
| Negativa | There isn't a pen | There aren't any books |
| Interrogativa | Is there...? | Are there...? |
| Respuesta corta | Yes, there is / No, there isn't | Yes, there are / No, there aren't |

#### ANY en negativas e interrogativas

- There aren't any chairs in the room.
- Are there any students in the classroom?
- I don't have any money.

## MÓDULO 5: POSESIÓN Y FAMILIA (Possession & Family)

### 5.1 HAVE GOT (Tener / Poseer)

> Have got es muy común en inglés británico y se usa igual que *have* para posesión.

#### Formas

| Sujeto | Afirmativa | Negativa | Interrogativa |
|---|---|---|---|
| I / You / We / They | have got | haven't got | Have I/you/we/they got...? |
| He / She / It | has got | hasn't got | Has he/she/it got...? |

#### Ejemplos

- I have got a new car. / I've got a new car.
- She has got blue eyes. / She's got blue eyes.
- They haven't got any children.
- Has he got a bike? Yes, he has. / No, he hasn't.

#### Diferencia: TO BE vs HAVE GOT

| Verbo | Uso | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| To Be | Descripción / Estado | I am tall. She is happy. |
| Have Got | Posesión / Atributo | I have got brown eyes. She has got a car. |

### 5.2 ADJETIVOS POSESIVOS (Possessive Adjectives)

| Adjetivo | Uso | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| my | mi / mis | My name is John |
| your | tu / tus / su / sus (usted) | Your book is red |
| his | su / sus (de él) | His car is new |
| her | su / sus (de ella) | Her mother is kind |
| its | su / sus (de ello) | Its tail is long |
| our | nuestro / nuestra | Our house is big |
| their | su / sus (de ellos) | Their children are smart |

Contraste con Pronombres Posesivos

| Adjetivo Posesivo | Pronombre Posesivo | Ejemplo Adjetivo | Ejemplo Pronombre |

| its | - | - | - |

| their | theirs | Their dog is small | The small dog is theirs |

### 5.3 SAXON GENITIVE ('s — Posesión con 's)

> Se usa para indicar que algo pertenece a una persona o animal.

#### Reglas

| Tipo | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| Singular | persona + 's + objeto | John's car |
| Plural (termina en s) | personas + ' + objeto | The students' books |
| Plural irregular | personas + 's + objeto | Children's toys |
| Nombres propios terminados en s | nombre + 's o solo ' | James' car / James's car |

#### Ejemplos

- My mother's sister is my aunt.
- The teacher's book is on the desk.
- Mary's house is very big.
- My parents' bedroom is upstairs.
