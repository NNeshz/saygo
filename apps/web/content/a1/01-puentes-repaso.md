# Módulo 1 — Puentes y Repaso Estratégico

*Cimentando el A1 · 6 horas*


**Diagnóstico activo de A1**

- Presente Simple vs. Continuo: ¿hábito o acción de ahora?
- Pasado Simple: narrar el día de ayer en 5 oraciones sin errores de DID
- Futuros: diferenciar plan (going to) de decisión/promesa espontánea (will)

**Vocabulario de identidad mexicana**

- Nacionalidad y origen: I am Mexican / I am from Mexico City / I come from Guadalajara
- Lugares: pyramid, colonial city, downtown/zócalo, plaza, cathedral, museum
- Comida: taco, tortilla, tamale, enchilada, mole, guacamole, salsa, spicy/hot, corn, avocado
- Celebraciones: Day of the Dead, posada, quinceañera (sweet fifteen), piñata, mariachi

**Errores comunes español → inglés**

- "I have years" → I am 15 years old
- "I am agree" → I agree / I don't agree
- "People is" → People are
- "Explain me" → Explain to me / Tell me
- "I am interesting" → I am interested
- "I have hunger/cold/heat" → I am hungry / I am cold / I am hot
- "Make me a favor" → Do me a favor
- "I go to the school every day" → I go to school every day (sin artículo)
- "I am having 15 years" → I am 15 years old (no continuo con estados)


## 1. ¿Para qué sirve esto?

En este módulo vas a consolidar todo lo que aprendiste en el nivel Zero/A1: los tiempos verbales esenciales, el vocabulario de tu mundo cotidiano mexicano y los errores más comunes que cometen los hispanohablantes al hablar inglés.
Antes de avanzar al A2, necesitas sentirte seguro usando el Present Simple, el Past Simple y las formas de hablar del futuro.
También aprenderás a corregir interferencias del español que confunden a los angloparlantes, como decir "I have 25 years" en lugar de "I am 25 years old".
Al terminar esta lección, tendrás una base sólida para todo el nivel A2.


## 2. Estructura y reglas

### (a) Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

| | **Present Simple** | **Present Continuous** |
|---|---|---|
| **Uso** | Hábitos, rutinas, hechos | Acciones en este momento / planes confirmados |
| **Marcadores** | always, usually, every day, sometimes, never, on Mondays | now, right now, at the moment, today, this week, Look! Listen! |
| **Forma** | Subject + base verb (he/she/it: +s/-es) | Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing |
| **Ejemplo (+)** | I eat tacos every Sunday. / She works in Guadalajara. | I am eating tacos right now. / She is working from home today. |
| **Ejemplo (−/?)** | They don't watch TV much. / Does he speak English? | They aren't watching TV — they're studying. / Is he speaking English now? |

> ⚠️ **Error frecuente:** Algunos verbos NO se usan en continuous: *know, like, love, want, need, believe, understand, have* (posesión).
> ❌ "I am knowing the answer." → ✓ "I know the answer."


### (b) Past Simple — Affirmative / Negative / Question

| | Afirmativa | Negativa | Pregunta |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Regular** | I walked to school. / She visited Oaxaca. | I didn't walk to school. / She didn't visit Oaxaca. | Did you walk to school? / Did she visit Oaxaca? |
| **Irregular** | We went to the market. / He ate pozole. / They had a great time. | We didn't go to the market. / He didn't eat pozole. / They didn't have fun. | Did you go to the market? / Did he eat pozole? / Did they have fun? |

> **Regla:** Usa DID para negativas y preguntas. El verbo principal va en su forma base.

> ⚠️ **Error frecuente:** No uses el pasado del verbo principal en negativas/preguntas.
> ❌ "She didn't went." → ✓ "She didn't go."
> ❌ "Did you ate?" → ✓ "Did you eat?"


### (c) Going to vs. Will

| | **Going to** | **Will** |
|---|---|---|
| **Uso** | Planes ya decididos / predicciones basadas en evidencia | Decisiones espontáneas / ofrecimientos / predicciones generales / promesas |
| **Forma** | am/is/are + *going to* + base verb | Subject + *will* + base verb |
| **Ejemplo 1** | I am going to visit my grandma on Saturday. *(plan previo)* | I will help you with that. *(decisión ahora)* |
| **Ejemplo 2** | Look at those clouds — it's going to rain. *(evidencia visible)* | I think it will rain next week. *(predicción general)* |
| **Ejemplo 3** | She's going to study medicine. | She will be a great doctor one day. |

> ⚠️ **Error frecuente:** No uses "will" para planes ya organizados.
> ❌ "I will go to my cousin's wedding on Saturday." (si ya tienes el plan)
> ✓ "I am going to go to my cousin's wedding on Saturday."

## 4. Ejemplos en contexto

1. My sister **goes** to the University of Guadalajara every Monday. (Mi hermana va a la Universidad de Guadalajara cada lunes.)
2. Right now, she **is studying** for her biology exam. (Ahora mismo, está estudiando para su examen de biología.)
3. Last weekend, we **went** to the market in Tlalpan and **bought** fresh vegetables. (El fin de semana pasado, fuimos al mercado en Tlalpan y compramos verduras frescas.)
4. My mom **didn't cook** yesterday because we ordered street food. (Mi mamá no cocinó ayer porque pedimos comida callejera.)
5. **Did** you **visit** the zócalo during the Independence Day celebrations? (¿Visitaste el zócalo durante las celebraciones del Día de Independencia?)
6. We **are going to make** tamales for Christmas — it's a family tradition. (Vamos a hacer tamales para Navidad — es una tradición familiar.)
7. I think it **will** be a beautiful Day of the Dead parade this year. (Creo que este año habrá un hermoso desfile del Día de Muertos.)
8. He **doesn't understand** English very well yet, but he **is learning** fast. (Él todavía no entiende muy bien el inglés, pero está aprendiendo rápido.)
9. My hometown **is** a small suburb outside of Monterrey — it's very quiet. (Mi ciudad natal es un suburbio pequeño a las afueras de Monterrey — es muy tranquilo.)
10. **Did** your family **celebrate** the Day of the Dead last November? (¿Tu familia celebró el Día de Muertos el noviembre pasado?)


## 5. Tabla de errores comunes

| ❌ Error | ✓ Corrección | Por qué |
|---|---|---|
| I have 25 years. | I am 25 years old. | En inglés la edad se expresa con "to be", no "to have". |
| I am agree with you. | I agree with you. | "Agree" es un verbo, no un adjetivo. No necesita "am/is/are". |
| People is very friendly here. | People are very friendly here. | "People" es plural en inglés; siempre lleva verbo plural. |
| She didn't went to school. | She didn't go to school. | Con "did/didn't" el verbo principal va en forma base, no en pasado. |
| I am very boring in class. | I am very bored in class. | "Boring" = aburrido (causa). "Bored" = aburrido (sentimiento). |
| He don't like coffee. | He doesn't like coffee. | Con he/she/it usamos "doesn't" en negativas. |
| I will go to the party tomorrow — I already bought my ticket. | I am going to go to the party tomorrow. | Planes decididos previamente usan "going to", no "will". |
| My English is very good, I study since three years. | I have studied English for three years. | Para acciones que empezaron en el pasado y continúan se usa Present Perfect + "for". |
| I have hunger. | I am hungry. | Las sensaciones físicas (hunger, thirst, fear) se expresan con "to be" en inglés. |


## 6. Comparación o contraste

### Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

| | Present Simple | Present Continuous |
|---|---|---|
| Describe | Rutina o hecho general | Acción en progreso ahora |
| Marcador típico | every day, always, usually | now, at the moment, look! |
| Forma | eat / eats | am/is/are + eating |
| Negativa | don't / doesn't + base | am not / isn't / aren't + -ing |

**4 pares de contraste:**

| Present Simple | Present Continuous |
|---|---|
| I **drink** coffee every morning. | I **am drinking** coffee right now. |
| She **works** at a bakery in Coyoacán. | She **is working** extra hours this week. |
| They **don't eat** meat. | They **aren't eating** meat this month either. |
| **Does** he **speak** Nahuatl? | **Is** he **speaking** Nahuatl right now? |


### Going to vs. Will

| | Going to | Will |
|---|---|---|
| Tipo de futuro | Plan previo / evidencia | Decisión espontánea / promesa / predicción general |
| Forma | am/is/are + going to + base | will + base verb |
| Ejemplo | I am going to visit Oaxaca next month. | I will call you later. |
| Cuándo se decide | Antes de hablar | En el momento de hablar |

**4 pares de contraste:**

| Going to | Will |
|---|---|
| We **are going to** watch the parade on Saturday. (plan) | Oh, it's starting! I **will** take a photo. (decisión ahora) |
| Look at those fireworks — it**'s going to** be a long night. (evidencia) | I think it **will** be a fun celebration. (predicción general) |
| She **is going to** study abroad next year. (ya lo decidió) | She **will** work very hard to get the scholarship. (promesa) |
| They **are going to** make mole for the party. (plan) | I **will** bring the drinks, then. (ofrecimiento espontáneo) |


## 7. Diálogo modelo

**Context: Two students talk before class at a high school in Mexico City.**


**Sofía:** Hey, Lucas! Are you coming to the school parade on Friday?

**Lucas:** Yes! I'm going to go with my family. We do it every year. Are you going?

**Sofía:** Of course. Last year's parade was amazing — did you see the giant puppets?

**Lucas:** I did! And the fireworks at night were incredible. I didn't sleep until midnight.

**Sofía:** Me neither. Right now I'm making a costume for my little sister. She wants to dress up as a skull for the Day of the Dead section.

**Lucas:** That's cool. My mom is going to make tamales for the celebration too.

**Sofía:** Will your whole family be there?

**Lucas:** Yes, everyone. My grandparents are coming from Puebla — it's their hometown.

**Sofía:** That's lovely. I think it will be even better this year.

**Lucas:** Agreed. See you on Friday!


**Traducción:**

**Sofía:** ¡Oye, Lucas! ¿Vas a venir al desfile escolar el viernes?

**Lucas:** ¡Sí! Voy a ir con mi familia. Lo hacemos cada año. ¿Tú vas?

**Sofía:** Claro. El desfile del año pasado fue increíble, ¿viste los títeres gigantes?

**Lucas:** ¡Sí! Y los fuegos artificiales de la noche fueron increíbles. No dormí hasta la medianoche.

**Sofía:** Yo tampoco. Ahora mismo estoy haciendo un disfraz para mi hermanita. Quiere disfrazarse de calavera para la sección del Día de Muertos.

**Lucas:** Qué padre. Mi mamá también va a hacer tamales para la celebración.

**Sofía:** ¿Va a estar toda tu familia?

**Lucas:** Sí, todos. Mis abuelos vienen de Puebla — es su ciudad natal.

**Sofía:** Qué bonito. Creo que este año va a ser aún mejor.

**Lucas:** De acuerdo. ¡Nos vemos el viernes!


## 8. Texto modelo

**My Favorite Mexican Celebration**

My favorite celebration is the Day of the Dead. Every year, my family decorates an altar with marigolds, photographs, and food. Last year, we visited the cemetery in our neighborhood and lit candles for my grandfather. It was a very emotional but beautiful night. Right now, I am planning the altar for this November. I am going to add my grandfather's favorite food — mole and tamales. I think it will be a wonderful celebration again. In my hometown, people always come together for this festivity. I believe it is one of the most meaningful traditions in Mexican culture, and I am proud to share it with the world.

*(101 palabras)*


**Traducción:**

Mi celebración favorita es el Día de Muertos. Cada año, mi familia decora un altar con cempasúchil, fotografías y comida. El año pasado, visitamos el cementerio de nuestra colonia y encendimos velas para mi abuelo. Fue una noche muy emotiva pero hermosa. Ahora mismo estoy planeando el altar para este noviembre. Voy a poner la comida favorita de mi abuelo: mole y tamales. Creo que va a ser una celebración maravillosa de nuevo. En mi ciudad natal, la gente siempre se reúne para esta festividad. Creo que es una de las tradiciones más significativas de la cultura mexicana, y estoy orgulloso/a de compartirla con el mundo.


## 9. Ejercicios de práctica

### Ejercicio 1 — Elige el tiempo correcto (Present Simple o Present Continuous)

Completa con la forma correcta del verbo en paréntesis.

1. My brother ________ (study) medicine at UNAM. He ________ (review) his notes right now.
2. We always ________ (eat) pozole on Independence Day, but tonight we ________ (have) tacos.
3. ________ she ________ (work) in Monterrey? No, she ________ (work) from home this month.
4. Look! The musicians ________ (play) in the zócalo. They usually ________ (play) on weekends.

### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 1

1. studies / is reviewing
2. eat / are having
3. Does … work / is working
4. are playing / play


### Ejercicio 2 — Pasado Simple: afirmativa, negativa y pregunta

Reescribe cada oración según la instrucción.

1. They visited the market. → **Negativa:** ________
2. She ate mole at the party. → **Pregunta:** ________
3. We didn't go to the parade. → **Afirmativa:** ________
4. He ________ (not / understand) the question. → Escribe la negativa.

### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 2

1. They didn't visit the market.
2. Did she eat mole at the party?
3. We went to the parade.
4. He didn't understand the question.


### Ejercicio 3 — Corrige los errores

Encuentra y corrige el error en cada oración.

1. I have 18 years old.
2. People is very helpful in my neighborhood.
3. She is agree with the teacher.
4. I am very boring in this class.
5. Did you went to the Day of the Dead celebration last year?

### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 3

1. I **am** 18 years old. *(edad → "to be")*
2. People **are** very helpful in my neighborhood. *(people = plural)*
3. She **agrees** with the teacher. *(agree es verbo, no adjetivo)*
4. I am very **bored** in this class. *(bored = sentimiento; boring = causa)*
5. Did you **go** to the Day of the Dead celebration last year? *(did + base verb)*


## 10. Resumen visual

| Concepto | Regla | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| Present Simple | Subject + base verb (he/she/it: +s) | She eats tamales every Christmas. |
| Present Continuous | Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing | She is eating right now. |
| Marcadores Simple | always, every day, usually, never | I usually drink atole in the morning. |
| Marcadores Continuous | now, at the moment, look!, this week | Look! It is raining. |
| Past Simple regular | verb + -ed | They visited Oaxaca last summer. |
| Past Simple negativa | didn't + base verb | He didn't go to the market. |
| Past Simple pregunta | Did + subject + base verb? | Did she eat pozole? |
| Going to | am/is/are + going to + base (plan previo) | I am going to visit my grandma. |
| Will | will + base (decisión espontánea) | I will bring the drinks! |
| Edad en inglés | I am X years old (nunca "I have") | I am 22 years old. |
