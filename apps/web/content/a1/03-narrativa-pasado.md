# Módulo 3 — Narrativa en el Pasado

*Pasado Continuo: was/were + -ing · 10 horas*


**Formación del Pasado Continuo**

- Afirmativo: I was studying / They were playing / She was working
- Negativo: I wasn't sleeping / We weren't watching TV
- Interrogativo: Were you listening? / Was he driving?
- Respuestas cortas: Yes, I was / No, they weren't

**Dos usos principales**

- Acción interrumpida: I was doing my homework when the lights went out
- Dos acciones simultáneas: While I was studying, my brother was playing video games
- Contexto de historia: It was raining. The wind was blowing. Suddenly...

**While vs. When**

- While + Pasado Continuo (acción en desarrollo): While I was walking...
- When + Pasado Simple (interrupción): ...when I saw my teacher

**Narrativa personal y académica**

- I was running in the hallway when I fell
- Everyone was silent. The teacher was watching us.
- During the posada, everyone was singing. Suddenly, the piñata broke.

**Vocabulario de narración**

- suddenly, immediately, at that moment, in the end, finally, luckily, unfortunately, while, during


## 1. ¿Para qué sirve esto?

El Pasado Continuo (*Past Continuous*) te permite contar historias de manera vívida y detallada.
Lo usas para describir qué estaba pasando *en un momento específico del pasado*, como el fondo de una escena.
También sirve para mostrar que dos acciones ocurrían al mismo tiempo, o que una acción fue interrumpida por otra.
Dominar este tiempo verbal hace que tus relatos en inglés suenen naturales y fluidos, no como una lista de eventos.


## 2. Estructura y reglas

### a) Afirmativo

| Sujeto | was / were | Verbo + -ing | Ejemplo |
|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|
| I | **was** | working | I was working. |
| He / She / It | **was** | sleeping | She was sleeping. |
| You | **were** | studying | You were studying. |
| We | **were** | eating | We were eating. |
| They | **were** | dancing | They were dancing. |

### b) Negativo

| Sujeto | was / were + not | Verbo + -ing | Ejemplo |
|--------|-----------------|--------------|---------|
| I | **was not** / **wasn't** | working | I wasn't working. |
| He / She / It | **was not** / **wasn't** | sleeping | She wasn't sleeping. |
| You / We / They | **were not** / **weren't** | studying | They weren't studying. |

### c) Pregunta

| Was / Were | Sujeto | Verbo + -ing | Ejemplo |
|-----------|--------|--------------|---------|
| **Was** | I / he / she / it | working? | Was she working? |
| **Were** | you / we / they | studying? | Were they studying? |

### d) Respuestas cortas

| Pregunta | Sí | No |
|----------|----|----|
| Was she sleeping? | Yes, she **was**. | No, she **wasn't**. |
| Were they dancing? | Yes, they **were**. | No, they **weren't**. |


### Reglas de ortografía para el -ing

| Regla | Descripción | Ejemplos |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Verbo termina en consonante-vocal-consonante (CVC) | Se duplica la consonante final | run → runn**ing**, sit → sitt**ing**, swim → swimm**ing** |
| Verbo termina en -e | Se elimina la -e | write → writ**ing**, make → mak**ing**, come → com**ing** |
| Verbo termina en -ie | Se cambia -ie por -y | lie → ly**ing**, die → dy**ing** |
| Todos los demás | Solo se añade -ing | eat → eat**ing**, play → play**ing**, work → work**ing** |

> **Nota CVC:** La regla aplica solo si la última sílaba está acentuada. "Listen" → listening (no doble) porque el acento está en "lis-".


### Usos principales del Past Continuous

| Uso | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| **Acción interrumpida** | Past Continuous + **when** + Past Simple | I was cooking **when** the phone rang. |
| **Acciones simultáneas** | **While** + Past Continuous, Past Continuous | **While** she was studying, he was playing. |
| **Descripción de fondo** | Past Continuous (sin interrupción) | The sun was shining and birds were singing. |
| **Contexto de historia** | Past Continuous al inicio del relato | It was raining and everyone was running. |


### ⚠️ Errores comunes de estructura

- ❌ I was cook dinner when she arrived. → ✓ I was **cooking** dinner when she arrived.
- ❌ While I studied, she was watching TV. → ✓ While I **was studying**, she was watching TV.
- ❌ They were dance at the party. → ✓ They were **dancing** at the party.

## 5. Tabla de errores comunes

| Error | Corrección | Explicación |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| ❌ I was work when she called. | ✓ I was **working** when she called. | Falta el -ing en el verbo principal. |
| ❌ While I was studying, she play music. | ✓ While I was studying, she **was playing** music. | La segunda acción también va en Past Continuous con "while". |
| ❌ They was dancing at the posada. | ✓ They **were** dancing at the posada. | Con "they" se usa "were", nunca "was". |
| ❌ I writting a message when the bus arrived. | ✓ I **was writing** a message when the bus arrived. | Se omitió "was" y se duplicó mal: write → writing (no writting). |
| ❌ When I was running, it rained. | ✓ When I was running, it **started to rain** / **rained**. (ambos válidos según el contexto) | "Rained" es correcto, pero el hablante suele confundir el tiempo del evento interrumptor. |
| ❌ She was not sleeping — she wasnot sleeping. | ✓ She **wasn't** sleeping. | La contracción correcta es "wasn't", no "wasnot". |
| ❌ Were he waiting for me? | ✓ **Was** he waiting for me? | Con "he" se usa "Was", no "Were". |
| ❌ We were already ate when you arrived. | ✓ We were already **eating** when you arrived. | Después de "were" va el gerundio (-ing), no el participio pasado. |


## 6. Comparación o contraste — While vs. When

El conector determina qué tiempo verbal usar en cada cláusula.

| Conector | Cláusula con conector | Cláusula principal | Explicación |
|----------|----------------------|--------------------|-------------|
| **While** | While I **was cooking** | she **arrived**. | "While" introduce la acción de fondo (continua). |
| **When** | When she **arrived** | I **was cooking**. | "When" introduce el evento puntual que interrumpe. |
| **While** | While he **was sleeping** | the alarm **went off**. | Acción larga + evento corto de interrupción. |
| **When** | When the alarm **went off** | he **was sleeping**. | El mismo contenido, perspectiva invertida con "when". |

> **Regla práctica:** "While" casi siempre va seguido de Past Continuous. "When" casi siempre va seguido de Past Simple.


## 7. Diálogo modelo

**Contexto:** Dos amigos hablan después de la posada navideña del barrio.


**Marco:** Hey, Luis! I was looking for you at the posada last night. Where were you?

**Luis:** Sorry! I was helping my aunt set up the decorations. We were running really late.

**Marco:** Ah, I see. Were you there when the piñata broke?

**Luis:** No! I was carrying the ponche from the kitchen at that moment. What happened?

**Marco:** It was so funny. While everyone was singing, the rope suddenly broke.

**Luis:** No way! Was anyone standing underneath?

**Marco:** Luckily, no. The kids were all watching from the side.

**Luis:** And what were the adults doing?

**Marco:** My dad was talking to the neighbors and my mom was taking photos. Nobody was paying attention!

**Luis:** That sounds like our family. Unfortunately, I always miss the best moments.

**Marco:** Don't worry. I filmed it. I was recording on my phone the whole time!

**Luis:** Finally, some good news!


**Traducción:**

**Marco:** ¡Oye, Luis! Te estaba buscando en la posada anoche. ¿Dónde estabas?

**Luis:** ¡Perdón! Estaba ayudando a mi tía a poner las decoraciones. Llegamos muy tarde.

**Marco:** Ah, ya veo. ¿Estabas ahí cuando se rompió la piñata?

**Luis:** ¡No! En ese momento estaba cargando el ponche desde la cocina. ¿Qué pasó?

**Marco:** Fue muy chistoso. Mientras todos cantaban, de repente se rompió el mecate.

**Luis:** ¡No puede ser! ¿Había alguien parado debajo?

**Marco:** Afortunadamente, no. Los niños estaban viendo desde un lado.

**Luis:** ¿Y qué estaban haciendo los adultos?

**Marco:** Mi papá estaba platicando con los vecinos y mi mamá estaba tomando fotos. ¡Nadie estaba poniendo atención!

**Luis:** Eso suena como nuestra familia. Desafortunadamente, siempre me pierdo los mejores momentos.

**Marco:** No te preocupes. Lo filmé. ¡Estaba grabando con mi celular todo el tiempo!

**Luis:** ¡Finalmente, buenas noticias!


## 8. Texto modelo

**The Night of the Posada**

Last December, our neighborhood held its annual posada. It was cold and the street lights were glowing orange. While the adults were setting up the tables, the children were running around and laughing. My cousin was playing the guitar and everyone was starting to gather around.

Suddenly, the piñata rope broke. At that moment, I was standing near the door talking to my friend. Immediately, the kids ran toward the candy that was falling everywhere. Unfortunately, my little brother slipped and fell, but luckily he was fine. Meanwhile, my mom was still arranging the food inside, completely unaware of the chaos.

Finally, everything calmed down. We ate, we sang, and we laughed. It was a night I will never forget.


**Traducción:**

La noche pasada en diciembre, nuestro barrio organizó su posada anual. Hacía frío y las luces de la calle brillaban en color naranja. Mientras los adultos acomodaban las mesas, los niños corrían y se reían. Mi primo tocaba la guitarra y todos empezaban a reunirse.

De repente, el mecate de la piñata se rompió. En ese momento, yo estaba parado cerca de la puerta hablando con mi amigo. Inmediatamente, los niños corrieron hacia los dulces que caían por todas partes. Desafortunadamente, mi hermanito resbaló y cayó, pero afortunadamente estaba bien. Mientras tanto, mi mamá seguía acomodando la comida adentro, completamente ajena al caos.

Finalmente, todo se calmó. Comimos, cantamos y reímos. Fue una noche que nunca olvidaré.


## 9. Ejercicios de práctica

### Ejercicio 1 — Completa las oraciones

Elige **was/were** y usa el verbo en **-ing**.

1. I ______ (study) when my phone ______ (ring).
2. They ______ (not / listen) when the teacher ______ (explain).
3. ______ she ______ (wait) for the bus when it ______ (start) to rain?
4. While he ______ (sleep), someone ______ (knock) on the door.
5. We ______ (eat) tacos when the lights ______ (go) out.

### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 1

1. I **was studying** when my phone **rang**.
2. They **weren't listening** when the teacher **explained**.
3. **Was** she **waiting** for the bus when it **started** to rain?
4. While he **was sleeping**, someone **knocked** on the door.
5. We **were eating** tacos when the lights **went** out.


### Ejercicio 2 — While o When

Elige **while** o **when** para completar cada oración.

1. ______ I was walking to school, I saw an accident.
2. She was cooking ______ the power went out.
3. ______ they were playing soccer, it started to rain.
4. He called me ______ I was in the middle of a meeting.
5. ______ we were waiting at the metro, a musician started playing.

### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 2

1. **While** I was walking to school, I saw an accident.
2. She was cooking **when** the power went out.
3. **While** they were playing soccer, it started to rain.
4. He called me **when** I was in the middle of a meeting.
5. **While** we were waiting at the metro, a musician started playing.


### Ejercicio 3 — Escribe tu propio relato

Escribe 5 oraciones sobre lo que estabas haciendo ayer a las 7 de la noche. Usa:
- al menos 2 oraciones con Past Continuous solo (descripción de fondo)
- al menos 1 oración con **while** + Past Continuous
- al menos 1 oración con **when** + Past Simple interrumpiendo

**Ejemplo de respuesta:**

> At 7 pm, I was sitting in the kitchen. My mom was making dinner and my brother was doing his homework. While I was watching TV, my dad arrived home. When he walked in, we were all talking at the same time. Suddenly, the dog knocked over the water bowl and everyone laughed.

### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 3

*(Las respuestas varían. Verifica que el estudiante haya usado:*
- *was/were + -ing correctamente*
- *while → Past Continuous en ambas cláusulas*
- *when → Past Simple para el evento que interrumpe)*


## 10. Resumen visual

| Elemento | Forma | Ejemplo |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Afirmativo (I/he/she/it) | **was** + verbo-ing | She was reading. |
| Afirmativo (you/we/they) | **were** + verbo-ing | They were talking. |
| Negativo | **wasn't / weren't** + verbo-ing | He wasn't sleeping. |
| Pregunta | **Was/Were** + sujeto + verbo-ing? | Were you waiting? |
| Respuesta corta sí | Yes, + sujeto + **was/were**. | Yes, I was. |
| Respuesta corta no | No, + sujeto + **wasn't/weren't**. | No, she wasn't. |
| Acción interrumpida | Past Continuous + **when** + Past Simple | I was cooking **when** she called. |
| Acciones simultáneas | **While** + Past Continuous + Past Continuous | **While** he slept, I worked. |
| CVC → doble consonante | consonante final ×2 + -ing | run → runn**ing** |
| Verbo en -e → quitar -e | -e + -ing | write → writ**ing** |
