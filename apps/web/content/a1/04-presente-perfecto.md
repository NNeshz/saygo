# Módulo 4 — Presente Perfecto

*Experiencias y cambios · have/has + participio · 12 horas*


**Formación**

- Afirmativo: I have visited / She has eaten / We have studied
- Negativo: I haven't been / He hasn't finished
- Interrogativo: Have you seen...? / Has she called...?
- Respuestas cortas: Yes, I have / No, he hasn't

**Participios irregulares esenciales**

- be → been / go → gone/been / see → seen / do → done / eat → eaten
- write → written / read → read / take → taken / give → given / find → found
- break → broken / choose → chosen / forget → forgotten / lose → lost

**Usos principales en A2**

- Experiencias de vida (sin tiempo específico): I have been to Cancún / Have you ever eaten tamales?
- Cambios recientes: She has changed a lot / My school has improved
- Acciones que empezaron y continúan: I have lived here for 5 years / She has studied English since 2020
- Acciones recientes con relevancia ahora: I have lost my ID card (and I still don't have it)

**For vs. Since vs. Ago — contraste sistemático**

- for + período de tiempo (duración) → Presente Perfecto: for two years, for six months
- since + punto de inicio → Presente Perfecto: since 2020, since Monday, since I was a child
- ago + Pasado Simple (distancia desde hoy): two years ago, a week ago

**Marcadores temporales clave**

- Ever / Never: Have you ever...? / I have never...
- Already / Yet: I have already finished / Have you finished yet?
- Just: I have just arrived
- Recently / Lately: I haven't seen her recently


## 1. ¿Para qué sirve esto?

El presente perfecto conecta el pasado con el presente: habla de experiencias de vida, de acciones recientes que siguen siendo relevantes ahora mismo y de situaciones que comenzaron en el pasado y todavía continúan.
Es uno de los tiempos más usados en inglés cotidiano, pero en español no tiene un equivalente exacto, por eso muchos hispanohablantes lo confunden con el pasado simple.
Aprenderlo bien te permite decir cosas como "He visitado tres países" o "Acabo de llegar" de forma natural y correcta en inglés.
Dominar este tiempo abre la puerta al inglés intermedio real: entrevistas de trabajo, conversaciones de viaje, trámites en el extranjero y mucho más.


## 2. Estructura y reglas

### (a) Afirmativa

| Sujeto | Auxiliar | Participio pasado | Complemento |
|--------|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| I / You / We / They | **have** | worked / eaten / gone | here before |
| He / She / It | **has** | worked / eaten / gone | here before |

- I have visited Oaxaca.
- She has eaten tacos al pastor.
- We have finished the report.

### (b) Negativa

| Sujeto | Auxiliar + not | Participio pasado |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| I / You / We / They | **have not** (haven't) | seen that movie |
| He / She / It | **has not** (hasn't) | seen that movie |

- I haven't seen that movie.
- He hasn't called me yet.

### (c) Pregunta

| Auxiliar | Sujeto | Participio pasado | Complemento |
|----------|--------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Have** | I / you / we / they | finished | the homework? |
| **Has** | he / she / it | finished | the homework? |

- Have you ever been to Cancún?
- Has she taken the exam?

### (d) Respuestas cortas

| Pregunta | Sí | No |
|----------|----|----|
| Have you finished? | Yes, I **have**. | No, I **haven't**. |
| Has he called? | Yes, he **has**. | No, he **hasn't**. |


### Participios irregulares esenciales

| Infinitivo | Pasado simple | Participio pasado |
|------------|---------------|-------------------|
| be | was / were | **been** |
| go | went | **gone** |
| see | saw | **seen** |
| do | did | **done** |
| eat | ate | **eaten** |
| write | wrote | **written** |
| take | took | **taken** |
| give | gave | **given** |
| find | found | **found** |
| break | broke | **broken** |
| forget | forgot | **forgotten** |
| lose | lost | **lost** |


### For / Since / Ago

| Palabra | Uso | Ejemplo |
|---------|-----|---------|
| **for** | duración (cuánto tiempo) | I have lived here **for** three years. |
| **since** | punto de inicio (desde cuándo) | I have lived here **since** 2021. |
| **ago** | tiempo pasado desde ahora (→ Past Simple) | I moved here three years **ago**. |

> **Regla clave:** `for` + período de tiempo · `since` + fecha o momento específico · `ago` siempre va con pasado simple, nunca con presente perfecto.


### ⚠️ Errores frecuentes de estructura

| ❌ Error | ✓ Correcto |
|----------|-----------|
| I have went to the gym. | I have **gone** to the gym. |
| She has **saw** the movie. | She has **seen** the movie. |
| Have you **ate** already? | Have you **eaten** already? |
| I have lost my keys **yesterday**. | I **lost** my keys yesterday. (→ Past Simple) |
| He have finished the work. | He **has** finished the work. |

## 5. Tabla de errores comunes

| # | ❌ Error | ✓ Correcto | Explicación |
|---|---------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | I have went to Puebla. | I have **gone** to Puebla. | El participio de "go" es "gone", no "went". |
| 2 | She has **saw** the doctor. | She has **seen** the doctor. | El participio de "see" es "seen". |
| 3 | I have lost my card **yesterday**. | I **lost** my card yesterday. | Con tiempo específico (yesterday) se usa Past Simple. |
| 4 | He **have** finished the form. | He **has** finished the form. | Con he/she/it el auxiliar es "has", no "have". |
| 5 | I am lived here since 2020. | I **have** lived here since 2020. | El presente perfecto usa "have/has", no "am/is/are". |
| 6 | Have you eaten **since** two hours? | Have you eaten **for** two hours? | "For" se usa con duración, "since" con punto de inicio. |
| 7 | I haven't **never** seen snow. | I **have never** seen snow. | No se usan dos negativos juntos en inglés. |
| 8 | She has **forgotted** her password. | She has **forgotten** her password. | "Forgotten" es el participio irregular correcto. |
| 9 | Have you **already** finished **yet**? | Have you finished **yet**? | "Already" va en afirmativas; "yet" en preguntas y negativas. |
| 10 | I have been in Mexico **ago** three years. | I moved to Mexico three years **ago**. | "Ago" siempre va al final y con Past Simple. |


## 6. Comparación o contraste

### Present Perfect vs. Past Simple

| | Present Perfect | Past Simple |
|-|-----------------|-------------|
| **Tiempo** | No específico / conectado al presente | Específico y terminado |
| **Marcadores típicos** | ever, never, already, yet, just, recently | yesterday, last week, in 2015, ago |
| **Pregunta clave** | ¿Tiene relevancia ahora? | ¿Cuándo pasó exactamente? |
| **Ejemplo ✓** | I have visited Cancún. (experiencia de vida) | I visited Cancún **last summer**. |

#### 4 pares contrastivos

| Present Perfect | Past Simple |
|-----------------|-------------|
| I **have lost** my keys. (las sigo buscando) | I **lost** my keys **yesterday**. (dato histórico) |
| **Have** you **eaten** tacos? (¿alguna vez?) | **Did** you **eat** tacos **last night**? |
| She **has lived** here for five years. (sigue viviendo) | She **lived** in Monterrey **from 2010 to 2015**. |
| We **have just** finished. (hace un momento) | We **finished** **at 3 p.m.** |


### For vs. Since vs. Ago

| Palabra | Pregunta que responde | Tiempo verbal | Ejemplo |
|---------|-----------------------|---------------|---------|
| **for** | ¿Por cuánto tiempo? | PP o PS | I have studied for two hours. |
| **since** | ¿Desde cuándo? | PP (siempre) | I have lived here since March. |
| **ago** | ¿Hace cuánto? | PS (siempre) | I moved here two years ago. |


## 7. Diálogo modelo

**Situación:** Valeria le pregunta a su compañero Mike sobre su experiencia viajando por México.


**Valeria:** Have you ever been to Mexico City?
**Mike:** Yes, I have! I've been there twice.
**Valeria:** Really? When did you go?
**Mike:** I went last year and in 2022.
**Valeria:** Have you visited Teotihuacán yet?
**Mike:** No, I haven't. I've never had time. Have you been there?
**Valeria:** Yes, I have. I went when I was a kid. It's amazing.
**Mike:** I've already bought my tickets for this trip. I'm going on Saturday.
**Valeria:** Great! Have you tried tlayudas?
**Mike:** Not yet. What are they?
**Valeria:** They're from Oaxaca. You have to try them!
**Mike:** I've just added Oaxaca to my list!


**Traducción:**

**Valeria:** ¿Alguna vez has ido a la Ciudad de México?
**Mike:** ¡Sí! He ido dos veces.
**Valeria:** ¿En serio? ¿Cuándo fuiste?
**Mike:** Fui el año pasado y en 2022.
**Valeria:** ¿Ya visitaste Teotihuacán?
**Mike:** No, todavía no. Nunca he tenido tiempo. ¿Tú has ido?
**Valeria:** Sí. Fui cuando era niña. Es increíble.
**Mike:** Ya compré mis boletos para este viaje. Voy el sábado.
**Valeria:** ¡Qué bien! ¿Has probado las tlayudas?
**Mike:** Todavía no. ¿Qué son?
**Valeria:** Son de Oaxaca. ¡Tienes que probarlas!
**Mike:** ¡Acabo de agregar Oaxaca a mi lista!


## 8. Texto modelo

**My Experience Learning English**

I have studied English for almost a year now, and I can already see a big difference. I have learned more than two hundred words, and I have written short texts every week. I have never taken a class abroad, but I have watched many shows in English with subtitles. Lately, I have been practicing with a language exchange partner I found online. We have talked about food, travel, and our jobs. I have already finished four modules of my course, and I haven't given up yet. Learning a new language has been one of the best decisions I have ever made.


**Traducción:**

He estudiado inglés durante casi un año y ya puedo ver una gran diferencia. He aprendido más de doscientas palabras y he escrito textos cortos cada semana. Nunca he tomado una clase en el extranjero, pero he visto muchas series en inglés con subtítulos. Últimamente he estado practicando con un compañero de intercambio de idiomas que encontré en línea. Hemos hablado de comida, viajes y trabajo. Ya terminé cuatro módulos de mi curso y todavía no me he rendido. Aprender un nuevo idioma ha sido una de las mejores decisiones que he tomado.


## 9. Ejercicios de práctica

### Ejercicio 1 — Completa con have/has y el participio correcto

Usa el verbo entre paréntesis en su forma de participio pasado.

1. She ________ ________ (see) that movie three times.
2. I ________ ________ (not / eat) breakfast yet.
3. ________ you ever ________ (be) to Guadalajara?
4. They ________ just ________ (find) a new apartment.
5. He ________ ________ (forget) his password again.


### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 1

1. She **has seen** that movie three times.
2. I **haven't eaten** breakfast yet.
3. **Have** you ever **been** to Guadalajara?
4. They **have** just **found** a new apartment.
5. He **has forgotten** his password again.


### Ejercicio 2 — ¿Present Perfect o Past Simple?

Elige la opción correcta.

1. I (have visited / visited) Oaxaca **last month**.
2. (Have you ever tried / Did you try) mole in your life?
3. She (has called / called) me **at 8 a.m. this morning**.
4. We (have lived / lived) here **since** 2020.
5. They (have gone / went) to the concert **two days ago**.


### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 2

1. I **visited** Oaxaca last month. (tiempo específico → Past Simple)
2. **Have you ever tried** mole in your life? (experiencia de vida → Present Perfect)
3. She **called** me at 8 a.m. this morning. (hora específica → Past Simple)
4. We **have lived** here since 2020. (desde un punto en el tiempo → Present Perfect)
5. They **went** to the concert two days ago. (ago → Past Simple)


### Ejercicio 3 — For, since o ago

Completa con **for**, **since** o **ago**.

1. I have worked here ________ three years.
2. She started this job six months ________.
3. We have been friends ________ high school.
4. He hasn't slept well ________ a week.
5. I last visited my grandparents two months ________.


### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 3

1. I have worked here **for** three years.
2. She started this job six months **ago**.
3. We have been friends **since** high school.
4. He hasn't slept well **for** a week.
5. I last visited my grandparents two months **ago**.

| Concepto | Forma | Ejemplo |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Afirmativa | S + have/has + participio | I have eaten. |
| Negativa | S + haven't/hasn't + participio | She hasn't called. |
| Pregunta | Have/Has + S + participio? | Have you seen it? |
| Respuesta corta | Yes, I have. / No, I haven't. | — |
| Experiencia de vida | ever / never | Have you ever been to London? |
| Acción muy reciente | just | I have just arrived. |
| Completado | already (afirm.) / yet (neg. y preg.) | I've already paid. / Not yet. |
| Duración | for + período | for two years |
| Punto de inicio | since + momento | since Monday |
| Tiempo pasado (PS) | ago + Past Simple | two years ago → I moved. |
