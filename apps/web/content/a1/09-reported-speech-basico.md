# Módulo 9 — Reported Speech Básico

*Say, Tell, Ask · 8 horas*


**Say vs. Tell vs. Ask**

- Say (sin objeto indirecto obligatorio): He said (that) he was tired
- Tell + objeto indirecto: He told me (that) he was tired / She told us a story
- Ask: He asked me if I was ready / She asked where the bathroom was

**Reported Speech con THAT (opcional)**

- "I am sick" → He said (that) he was sick
- "I will call you" → She said (that) she would call me
- "I have finished" → He said (that) he had finished

**Cambios básicos (backshift)**

- Presente Simple → Pasado Simple: "I live here" → He said he lived there
- Will → Would: "I will go" → She said she would go
- Can → Could: "I can swim" → He said he could swim
- Cambios de pronombres y adverbios: now → then, today → that day, here → there, this → that

**Preguntas reportadas**

- Yes/No: "Are you Mexican?" → He asked if I was Mexican
- WH-: "Where do you live?" → She asked where I lived (sin inversión)

**Órdenes y peticiones reportadas**

- "Sit down" → The teacher told us to sit down
- "Don't be late" → He told me not to be late

**Verbos de reporte adicionales**

- advise + to: The doctor advised me to rest
- warn + not to: He warned me not to forget my ID
- suggest + -ing: She suggested taking the metro
- remind + to: She reminded me to bring the receipt


## 1. ¿Para qué sirve esto?

En la vida real constantemente repetimos lo que alguien dijo: "Mi jefe me dijo que llegara temprano" o "Mi mamá preguntó que dónde estaba."
El *reported speech* (estilo indirecto) es la herramienta que usamos en inglés para transmitir esos mensajes sin citar palabra por palabra.
Dominar esto te permite reportar conversaciones, instrucciones y preguntas con naturalidad — en el trabajo, la escuela y los trámites diarios.
En este módulo aprenderás los verbos clave (*say*, *tell*, *ask*), cómo cambian los tiempos verbales y cómo transformar preguntas y órdenes.


## 2. Estructura y reglas

### (a) Say / Tell / Ask — comparación general

| Verbo | ¿Necesita objeto? | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-------------------|------------|---------|
| **say** | No | say + (that) + clause | She **said** (that) she was tired. |
| **tell** | Sí (persona) | tell + person + (that) + clause | She **told me** (that) she was tired. |
| **ask** | Opcional | ask + (person) + question / to-inf | He **asked** (me) where I lived. |

> ⚠️ "She said me" ❌ — *say* nunca lleva objeto de persona directamente.
> ⚠️ "She told that she was tired" ❌ — *tell* siempre necesita la persona: *told me*.


### (b) Backshift — cambio de tiempos verbales

Cuando el verbo principal está en pasado, los tiempos dentro del reported speech retroceden un paso.

| Tiempo en discurso directo | Tiempo en reported speech | Ejemplo directo | Ejemplo reportado |
|---------------------------|--------------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Present Simple | Past Simple | "I **work** here." | She said she **worked** there. |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous | "I **am working**." | He said he **was working**. |
| Past Simple | Past Perfect | "I **finished** it." | She said she **had finished** it. |
| Present Perfect | Past Perfect | "I **have seen** it." | He said he **had seen** it. |
| **will** | **would** | "I **will** call you." | She said she **would** call me. |
| **can** | **could** | "I **can** help." | He said he **could** help. |
| **may** | **might** | "It **may** rain." | She said it **might** rain. |

> ⚠️ *Must* puede quedarse igual o convertirse en *had to* para obligación pasada.


### (c) Cambios de pronombres y expresiones de tiempo/lugar

| Discurso directo | Reported speech |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| I | he / she |
| we | they |
| my | his / her |
| you (sing.) | I / he / she |
| here | there |
| now | then |
| today | that day |
| yesterday | the day before / the previous day |
| tomorrow | the next day / the following day |
| this | that |
| these | those |
| last week | the week before |


### (d) Reported questions

#### Yes/No questions → use **if** or **whether** (sin inversión)

**Directo:** "Are you coming?"
**Reportado:** She asked **if** I was coming. / She asked **whether** I was coming.

**Directo:** "Do you have an appointment?"
**Reportado:** He asked whether I had an appointment.

> ⚠️ No hay inversión sujeto-auxiliar en reported questions.
> ⚠️ "He asked if was I coming" ❌

#### WH- questions → keep the WH-word, normal word order (sin inversión)

**Directo:** "Where do you live?" → **Reportado:** She asked where **I lived**.

**Directo:** "What time does it start?" → **Reportado:** He asked what time **it started**.

**Directo:** "Why are you late?" → **Reportado:** She asked why **I was late**.

> ⚠️ "She asked where did I live" ❌ — después del WH-word el orden es sujeto + verbo.


### (e) Reported orders — tell + to-infinitive

| Tipo | Estructura | Ejemplo directo | Ejemplo reportado |
|------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Afirmativa | tell + person + **to** + inf | "Open the door." | She told him **to open** the door. |
| Negativa | tell + person + **not to** + inf | "Don't be late." | He told me **not to be** late. |


### (f) Otros verbos de reporte frecuentes

| Verbo | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **advise** | advise + person + **to** + inf | She advised me **to study** every day. |
| **warn** | warn + person + **not to** + inf | He warned us **not to** park there. |
| **suggest** | suggest + **-ing** / suggest + that | She suggested **taking** the bus. / She suggested (that) we take the bus. |
| **remind** | remind + person + **to** + inf | He reminded me **to bring** my ID. |

> ⚠️ "She suggested to go" ❌ → "She suggested going" ✓

## 5. Tabla de errores comunes

| Error | Incorrecto ❌ | Correcto ✓ | Explicación |
|-------|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| *say* con objeto de persona | She said me she was tired. | She **told me** she was tired. | *Say* nunca lleva objeto de persona. |
| *tell* sin objeto de persona | She told that she was ready. | She **told me** that she was ready. | *Tell* siempre necesita la persona. |
| Inversión en reported question | He asked where did I live. | He asked where **I lived**. | Las reported questions usan orden normal S+V. |
| Inversión en yes/no question | She asked if was he coming. | She asked if **he was** coming. | No se invierte en reported speech. |
| *suggest* + to-infinitive | She suggested to leave early. | She suggested **leaving** early. | *Suggest* va seguido de -ing (o that-clause). |
| Sin backshift | He said he **works** here. | He said he **worked** here. | Si el verbo intro está en pasado, hay que aplicar backshift. |
| *ask* usado como *tell* para reportar órdenes | She asked me open the door. | She **told me to** open the door. | Las órdenes usan *tell + to-infinitive*. |
| Pronombre sin cambiar | "I am tired." → She said I was tired. | She said **she** was tired. | Los pronombres cambian según la perspectiva. |


## 6. Comparación o contraste

### Say vs. Tell vs. Ask

| | **say** | **tell** | **ask** |
|-|---------|----------|---------|
| ¿Objeto obligatorio? | No | Sí (persona) | No (opcional) |
| Uso principal | Declaraciones | Información a alguien | Preguntas / peticiones |
| ¿Puede ir con *that*? | Sí | Sí | No (usa if/whether/WH) |
| Ejemplo | She **said** she was busy. | She **told me** she was busy. | She **asked** if I was busy. |

### Discurso directo vs. Reportado — lado a lado

| Discurso directo | Reported speech |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| "I **am** hungry," he said. | He said he **was** hungry. |
| "We **will** be there," they said. | They said they **would** be there. |
| "**Can** you call me?" she asked. | She asked if I **could** call her. |
| "**Don't** touch that," the teacher said. | The teacher told us **not to** touch that. |


## 7. Diálogo modelo

**Setting:** Ana calls her friend Sofía after talking to their English teacher, Mr. Ramírez.

> **Sofía:** Hey, what did Mr. Ramírez say after class?
>
> **Ana:** He said our pronunciation was improving a lot.
>
> **Sofía:** Really? Did he mention the exam?
>
> **Ana:** Yes. He told us that the exam would be on Friday.
>
> **Sofía:** Oh no. Did he say what topics it would cover?
>
> **Ana:** He said it would cover reported speech and the past perfect.
>
> **Sofía:** Did he give any advice?
>
> **Ana:** He advised us to review our notes from that week.
>
> **Sofía:** And did he warn us about anything?
>
> **Ana:** He warned us not to leave everything for the last minute.
>
> **Sofía:** Good tip. Did anyone ask about extra credit?
>
> **Ana:** Yes, Carlos asked if there was extra credit, but Mr. Ramírez said there wasn't.

**Traducción:**

> **Sofía:** Oye, ¿qué dijo el señor Ramírez después de clase?
>
> **Ana:** Dijo que nuestra pronunciación estaba mejorando mucho.
>
> **Sofía:** ¿En serio? ¿Mencionó el examen?
>
> **Ana:** Sí. Nos dijo que el examen sería el viernes.
>
> **Sofía:** Ay no. ¿Dijo qué temas cubriría?
>
> **Ana:** Dijo que cubriría el reported speech y el past perfect.
>
> **Sofía:** ¿Nos dio algún consejo?
>
> **Ana:** Nos aconsejó repasar los apuntes de esa semana.
>
> **Sofía:** ¿Y nos advirtió algo?
>
> **Ana:** Nos advirtió que no dejáramos todo para el último momento.
>
> **Sofía:** Buen consejo. ¿Alguien preguntó por crédito extra?
>
> **Ana:** Sí, Carlos preguntó si había crédito extra, pero el señor Ramírez dijo que no.


## 8. Texto modelo

**My Neighbor's Advice**

Last week I talked to my neighbor Doña Lupita, who has lived in the United States for many years. She told me that English was not as difficult as I thought. She said she had struggled at first, but that practice made everything easier. She advised me to watch series in English with subtitles and to talk to myself out loud every day. She also warned me not to be afraid of making mistakes, because that was how she had learned. When I asked her which level had been the hardest, she said it had been the intermediate stage. Before she left, she reminded me to be patient with myself. Her words gave me a lot of motivation.

**Traducción:**

La semana pasada hablé con mi vecina Doña Lupita, quien ha vivido en Estados Unidos por muchos años. Me dijo que el inglés no era tan difícil como yo pensaba. Dijo que al principio le había costado trabajo, pero que la práctica hacía todo más fácil. Me aconsejó ver series en inglés con subtítulos y hablarme a mí mismo en voz alta todos los días. También me advirtió que no le tuviera miedo a cometer errores, porque así había aprendido ella. Cuando le pregunté cuál nivel había sido el más difícil, dijo que había sido la etapa intermedia. Antes de irse, me recordó que fuera paciente conmigo mismo. Sus palabras me dieron mucha motivación.


## 9. Ejercicios de práctica

### Ejercicio 1 — Transforma al reported speech

Rewrite each sentence as reported speech. Pay attention to backshift and pronoun changes.

1. "I am very tired today." (She said…)
2. "We will finish the project tomorrow." (They said…)
3. "I can't come to the party." (He said…)
4. "I have already eaten." (She said…)
5. "Don't open that window." (The teacher told us…)

### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 1

1. She said she was very tired that day.
2. They said they would finish the project the following day.
3. He said he couldn't come to the party.
4. She said she had already eaten.
5. The teacher told us not to open that window.


### Ejercicio 2 — Reported questions

Transform each direct question into a reported question.

1. "Are you a student?" (She asked me…)
2. "Where does your brother work?" (He asked me…)
3. "What time does the office open?" (She asked…)
4. "Can I bring a friend?" (He asked…)
5. "Why were you late?" (The teacher asked me…)

### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 2

1. She asked me if I was a student.
2. He asked me where my brother worked.
3. She asked what time the office opened.
4. He asked if he could bring a friend.
5. The teacher asked me why I had been late.


### Ejercicio 3 — Elige el verbo correcto y la estructura correcta

Fill in the blank with the correct form of *say*, *tell*, *ask*, *advise*, *warn*, *suggest*, or *remind*.

1. She _______ me that the meeting was at three o'clock. *(decirle a alguien)*
2. He _______ that he was happy with the results. *(decir)*
3. The doctor _______ me not to eat too much sugar. *(advertir)*
4. My teacher _______ practicing every day. *(sugerir)*
5. He _______ whether I had done my homework. *(preguntar)*
6. She _______ me to bring my passport. *(recordar)*

### ✅ Respuestas — Ejercicio 3

1. She **told** me that the meeting was at three o'clock.
2. He **said** that he was happy with the results.
3. The doctor **warned** me not to eat too much sugar.
4. My teacher **suggested** practicing every day.
5. He **asked** whether I had done my homework.
6. She **reminded** me to bring my passport.
