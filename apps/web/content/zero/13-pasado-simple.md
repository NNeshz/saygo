# Pasado Simple

**TO BE + Regulares + Irregulares + There was/were**

*Hablando del ayer — 14 a 16 horas*

---

## TO BE en pasado: was / were

| Pronombre | Pasado |
|-----------|--------|
| I | **was** |
| You | **were** |
| He / She / It | **was** |
| We / They | **were** |

### Afirmativo, negativo, interrogativo

| | Afirmativo | Negativo | Interrogativo |
|--|-----------|----------|--------------|
| I | I **was** happy. | I **wasn't** there. | **Was** I right? |
| You | You **were** late. | You **weren't** ready. | **Were** you there? |
| He/She | She **was** a nurse. | He **wasn't** home. | **Was** she ok? |
| We/They | They **were** students. | We **weren't** tired. | **Were** they late? |

**Respuestas cortas:**
```
Was she happy?     →  Yes, she was. / No, she wasn't.
Were they there?   →  Yes, they were. / No, they weren't.
```

---

## There was / There were

| | Singular | Plural |
|--|----------|--------|
| **Afirmativo** | There **was** a problem. | There **were** many people. |
| **Negativo** | There **wasn't** any time. | There **weren't** any cars. |
| **Interrogativo** | **Was** there a hospital? | **Were** there many students? |

```
There was a big party last night.    →  Hubo una gran fiesta anoche.
There weren't any taxis.             →  No había taxis.
Was there a problem?                 →  ¿Hubo algún problema?
```

## Verbos regulares en pasado: + ed

### Formación

| Terminación | Regla | Ejemplo |
|-------------|-------|---------|
| General | + **ed** | work → work**ed** |
| Termina en **e** | + **d** | live → live**d** |
| Consonante + **y** | **ied** | study → stud**ied** |
| CVC tónica | duplicar + **ed** | stop → stop**ped** |

### Pronunciación de -ed

| Sonido | Cuándo | Ejemplos |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **/t/** | después de consonante sorda | worked, watched, talked |
| **/d/** | después de vocal o consonante sonora | played, lived, called |
| **/ɪd/** | después de /t/ o /d/ | wanted, needed, started |

## Pasado Simple con DID — Negativo e interrogativo

Con DID, el verbo principal vuelve al **infinitivo** (sin -ed, sin cambio):

### Negativo: didn't + infinitivo

```
I didn't go.          (no *I didn't went.* ❌)
She didn't eat.       (no *She didn't ate.* ❌)
They didn't see us.
```

### Interrogativo: Did + sujeto + infinitivo

| Pregunta | Resp. afirmativa | Resp. negativa |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Did you go? | Yes, I did. | No, I didn't. |
| Did she eat? | Yes, she did. | No, she didn't. |
| Did they come? | Yes, they did. | No, they didn't. |

> **Regla clave:** DID ya lleva el pasado — el verbo vuelve al infinitivo: *Did he **go**?* ✓ (no *Did he **went**?* ❌)

## WH- Questions en pasado

```
WH-  +  did  +  sujeto  +  infinitivo  +  ?

Where   did   you      go     last weekend?
What    did   she      eat    for dinner?
Who     did   you      see    yesterday?
How     did   they     travel to Rome?
When    did   he       arrive?
Why     did   you      leave early?
```

## Contraste presente / pasado

```
I am happy now.          →  Estoy contento ahora. (presente)
I was sad yesterday.     →  Estaba triste ayer. (pasado)

She works at a hospital. →  Trabaja en un hospital. (presente)
She worked there for 5 years. →  Trabajó allí 5 años. (pasado)

It is sunny today.       →  Hoy está soleado.
It was cloudy last weekend. →  El fin de semana pasado estuvo nublado.
```

## Diálogo modelo

```
A: Did you have a good weekend?
B: Yes, I did! I went to the beach with my family.
A: Oh really? Was the weather nice?
B: Yes, it was beautiful. It was sunny and warm.
A: What did you do there?
B: We swam, ate seafood and took a lot of photos. What about you?
A: I stayed home. I was tired.
B: Did you watch anything?
A: Yes, I watched a great film. Did you know the one called...?
```
