# Tiempo y Planificación

**Futuros: BE GOING TO + WILL**

*Hablando del mañana — 8 a 10 horas*

---

## BE GOING TO — Intenciones y predicciones con evidencia

Se usa para hablar de **planes decididos con anterioridad** o **predicciones basadas en evidencia visible**:

### Formación

```
Sujeto  +  am/is/are  +  going to  +  infinitivo

I       am going to   study medicine.
He      is going to   travel next year.
They    are going to  get married.
```

### Negativo

```
I'm not going to eat meat anymore.
She isn't going to come to the party.
They aren't going to move.
```

### Interrogativo

| Pregunta | Resp. afirmativa | Resp. negativa |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Are you going to study? | Yes, I am. | No, I'm not. |
| Is he going to come? | Yes, he is. | No, he isn't. |
| Are they going to travel? | Yes, they are. | No, they aren't. |

### Uso: plan decidido antes

```
I'm going to buy a new car next month.  →  (ya lo decidí antes)
She's going to study medicine.          →  (es su plan)
We're going to visit my grandparents.   →  (plan establecido)
```

### Uso: predicción con evidencia visual

```
Look at those clouds! It's going to rain.   →  (veo las nubes ahora)
She's very pale. She's going to faint.      →  (evidencia visual)
Be careful! You're going to fall!           →  (lo estoy viendo)
```

## WILL — Decisiones espontáneas y promesas

Se usa para **decisiones tomadas en el momento** y **promesas**:

### Formación

```
Sujeto  +  will ('ll)  +  infinitivo

I       will open    the window.   →  I'll open the window.
He      will help    you.          →  He'll help you.
They    will come    tomorrow.     →  They'll come tomorrow.
```

### Negativo: won't (will not)

```
I won't tell anyone. (promesa)
She won't be late.
They won't forget.
```

### Interrogativo

| Pregunta | Resp. afirmativa | Resp. negativa |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Will you help? | Yes, I will. | No, I won't. |
| Will she come? | Yes, she will. | No, she won't. |

### Uso: decisión espontánea (en el momento)

```
[El teléfono suena]   →  I'll get it!          (decido ahora)
[Alguien necesita ayuda]  →  I'll help you!   (oferta espontánea)
[En la tienda]  →  I'll take the blue one.     (decido en ese momento)
```

### Uso: promesas

```
I'll call you tomorrow.   (prometo llamarte)
I won't forget.           (prometo no olvidar)
I'll always be here.      (promesa)
```

---

## Contraste clave: GOING TO vs. WILL

| Situación | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Plan decidido **antes** | going to | I'm going to buy a car. (ya lo decidí) |
| Decisión **en el momento** | will | I'll take this one! (lo decido ahora) |
| Predicción con **evidencia** | going to | It's going to rain. (veo las nubes) |
| Predicción / opinión **general** | will | I think it will be a good year. |
| **Promesa** | will | I'll be there. / I won't tell. |

```
[Planificado]:  I'm going to study medicine. (ya lo decidí)
[Espontáneo]:   The phone is ringing. I'll get it! (decido ahora)

[Con evidencia]:Those clouds! It's going to rain. (lo veo)
[Opinión]:      I think it will rain this week. (predicción general)
```

## Shall I / Shall we — Sugerencias formales

*Shall* se usa para hacer **sugerencias** u **ofrecimientos** de forma más formal o cortés:

```
Shall I open the window?    →  ¿Abro la ventana? (ofrezco)
Shall we go to the cinema?  →  ¿Vamos al cine? (sugerencia)
Shall I call a taxi?        →  ¿Llamo un taxi?
What shall we do tonight?   →  ¿Qué hacemos esta noche?
```

> *Shall* es más formal que *Let's* o *Should*. En inglés americano es poco común — se prefiere *Should I...?* o *Let's...* En inglés británico es habitual.

## Diálogo modelo

```
A: What are you going to do this weekend?
B: I'm going to visit my family. We're going to have a big lunch.
   What about you?
A: I'm not sure yet. Maybe I'll go to the cinema.
B: Oh, what will you watch?
A: I don't know. I'll decide when I get there.
B: Look at those clouds — it's going to rain this afternoon.
A: You're right. Shall I take an umbrella?
B: Definitely! I think it'll rain all day.
```
